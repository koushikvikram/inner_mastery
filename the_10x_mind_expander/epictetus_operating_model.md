# Epictetus Operating Model — Dan Sullivan, *The 10x Mind Expander*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Dan Sullivan, *The 10x Mind Expander*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: 10x is **easier than 2x** — 2x can be brute-forced (longer hours, late nights), and so it never demands the structural overhaul that produces freedom; 10x is so massive it *cannot* be done by working harder, which forces a different way. The mechanism is **Mental Time Travel** — speak about 10x as if it has *already happened*, then look back at today as the past. Confidence is the *output*, not the input — drive growth through the **4 C’s**: **Commitment → Courage → Capability → Confidence**. Look at **Historical Proof** (you have already 10x’d several times). Build **Unique Ability Teamwork** ("Grow or Go"); treat hires and tools as **Multipliers**, not costs. **Technology = teamwork made automatic** — geared for exponential outputs, dangerous for linear minds. Shed the **9 dead mindsets** (linear, settling down, "enough," blame, permission, control of exponentials, negative relationships, courage-free future, "giving back"). Use the **Number 21 trick** to defuse social judgment. Final paradox: **exponential results take less time than linear ones.**

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Replace 2x linear plodding with 10x structural thinking.
- Speak from the **future-as-already-happened**, not the present-trying-to-reach-it.
- Run the **4 C’s** loop: commit before confidence; let capability build it.
- Treat people and technology as **multipliers**, never as costs.
- Shed the 9 dead mindsets that quietly anchor you to linear life.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Frame goals as 10x, not 2x** — 2x defaults to "more work."
- **Mind-Travel daily** — write/speak from the future tense having already arrived.
- **Commit before knowing how** — courage flows from commitment, not the other way.
- **Hire / automate where the multiplier is ≥ 10x your cost.**
- **Run the Number 21 trick** any time social judgment slows you.

### Routines / checklists

- **10x reframe (start of any new goal)**
  - Replace **"How do I 2x?"** with **"What would 10x require I stop doing?"**
  - 2x prompts harder work. 10x prompts a different *who*, *how*, *when*, and *with what*.
  - Outcome: a goal that cannot be brute-forced.

- **Mental Time Travel exercise (weekly, 20 min)**
  - Set the year: 10 years out, 10x bigger life.
  - Write in **past tense** as if you’re looking back:
    - *"What did I delegate?"*
    - *"What technologies did I adopt?"*
    - *"How did my relationships change?"*
    - *"Which 9 mindsets did I shed?"*
  - The exercise is invalid if you slip into future tense — restart.

- **4 C’s sequence (per major bet)**
  - **Commitment** — declare the 10x goal before you have the answers.
  - **Courage** — stay in the discomfort the commitment provokes.
  - **Capability** — let new skills, systems, and people emerge from solving.
  - **Confidence** — only now; don’t demand it earlier.
  - When stuck, ask: *which C am I in, and which one am I refusing to enter?*

- **Historical Proof scan (when 10x feels impossible)**
  - List 3 prior 10x leaps (income, skill, vocabulary, network, geography).
  - Note: today’s "ordinary" was once unimaginable.
  - Use it as evidence, not nostalgia: the 10x mechanism has worked in your life before.

- **Multiplier audit (monthly)**
  - For each cost (hire, tool, subscription), compute: revenue / freedom unlocked vs. cost.
  - If multiplier ≥ 10x, treat it as **investment**, not expense.
  - The unhired assistant who would free $25K of revenue is not a $3K cost — it is a $22K loss.

- **Unique Ability + Grow-or-Go check (quarterly, per role)**
  - Is each person on **work they’re passionate about and feel hero in**?
  - Is the team **alert and resourceful** — searching for faster, bigger?
  - If neither: the role needs reshaping or the person needs rotation.

- **Technology-as-Teamwork pass (quarterly)**
  - For each significant manual workflow, ask: *what is the automation that makes this teamwork-on-autopilot?*
  - Pick one. Implement. Compound.
  - Stale workflow = compounding tax against your 10x.

- **Shed the 9 dead mindsets (monthly review)**
  - Linear / settling down / "enough" / blame / seeking permission / controlling exponentials / negative relationships / courage-free future / "giving back" (replace with *making things better*).
  - Pick the one most active in your life right now. Plan one explicit move to dismantle it.

- **Number 21 trick (in moments of social hesitation)**
  - Assume you are the **21st most important person** in anyone else’s life.
  - They aren’t judging you — they aren’t even thinking about you.
  - Make the move.

- **Shrink-the-past extraction (quarterly, 30 min)**
  - Take a long phase (job, project, era).
  - Distill it into 3–5 reusable principles or moves (Sullivan distilled 4 yrs of college into 5 steps).
  - Keep the wisdom, drop the weight.

### What good execution looks like
- Goals are 10x; 2x feels like timidity, not realism.
- You speak about your 10x life in past tense in private journaling.
- Big bets get committed *before* you have the plan.
- Hires and tools are evaluated by multiplier, not by line item.
- Each quarter, one of the 9 dead mindsets is visibly dismantled.
- Social-judgment hesitation lasts seconds, not weeks.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **2x compels brute force; 10x compels structural reinvention.** The "easier" path is the bigger one.
- **Brains specialize in the ordinary** — Mind Travel makes the extraordinary ordinary.
- **Confidence is a lagging indicator**: it’s the *output* of the 4 C’s, not the prerequisite.
- **You have 10x’d before**: the mechanism is proven by your own history.
- **People and tech are multipliers** — measured against the freedom they unlock, not the cost they list.
- **The universe is non-equilibrium**: 10x aligns with reality’s direction; "settled" fights it.
- **Exponential results consume less time than linear ones** — at scale, the right work is the lighter work.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If a goal can be reached by working harder, it isn’t 10x.
- If you’re waiting for confidence, you’re skipping commitment — start in the wrong place.
- If the multiplier is ≥ 10x, the cost is irrelevant.
- If the workflow is manual at scale, technology debt is compounding silently.
- If you can name which of the 9 dead mindsets is running you, you’re halfway out.
- If social judgment is delaying a move, run Number 21 and proceed.

---

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **10x-framed goals / quarter** (vs. 2x).
- **Mind-Travel sessions / month** (in past tense).
- **4 C’s tracker** (current C per major commitment).
- **Multiplier audits / quarter** (and decisions that followed).
- **Dead-mindsets shed / quarter** (with specific dismantling move).
- **Number 21 invocations / week** (where it actually unblocked action).

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: did I think 2x or 10x in this week’s decisions?
- **Monthly**: which C am I stuck in? Which dead mindset is most active?
- **Quarterly**: refresh Historical Proof, run multiplier audit, write a new "shrink-the-past" extraction.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re *talking* 10x but *operating* 2x, the model has failed. The diagnostic is brutal: did your last big move require harder work, or different work? If harder — you wrote a 2x check in a 10x book. Restart from a Mind Travel session, in past tense.
