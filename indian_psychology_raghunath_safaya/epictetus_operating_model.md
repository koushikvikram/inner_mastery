# Epictetus Operating Model — Indian Psychology (Raghunath Safaya)

> Source frame: psychology is the *outcome of the science and philosophy of the Self*. The Self (*Atman*) is pure consciousness; the mind (*Manas / Antahkarana*) is *subtle matter* — an instrument. The "I" is the witness, not the contents. Knowledge has three legitimate sources: *Pratyaksha* (perception), *Anumana* (inference), and *Sabda / Pratibha* (testimony / intuition). Consciousness pervades waking, dream, deep sleep, and *Turiya* — and the "I" persists across all four.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- **Operate from the witness**: stop identifying with the contents of the mind; treat the mind as a tool.
- **Triangulate knowledge**: use perception + inference + testimony/intuition before committing.
- **Read all four states as data**: waking quality, dream patterns, sleep recovery, and moments of pure presence.
- **Reduce the production of harmful samskaras**: change the impressions you’re depositing today — they *are* tomorrow’s reactions.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Witness check**: short pauses where you locate yourself as the *observer* of thought, not the thought.
- **Antahkarana hygiene**: end-of-day pass over Manas (sensory load), Ahamkara (where you took things personally), Buddhi (decisions made with discernment vs autopilot).
- **Three-source decision rule**: refuse to commit on a single epistemology when stakes are real.
- **Treat sleep & dream as part of the data set**, not a black box.

### Routines / checklists

- **Witness check (3× / day, 60 seconds)**
  - Pause. Notice one thought, mood, or impulse.
  - Re-state: *"This is a current content of the instrument. I am the one observing it."*
  - Resume action without acting from the fusion.

- **Antahkarana hygiene (end of day, 5–7 minutes)**
  - **Manas**: any unprocessed sensory load (noise, screens, conversations)?
  - **Ahamkara**: where did I take something personally today (criticism, status, comparison)?
  - **Buddhi**: name one decision made with clear discernment, and one made on autopilot.

- **Three-source decision drill (per significant decision)**
  - **Pratyaksha**: what did I directly observe?
  - **Anumana**: what does inference / data conclude?
  - **Sabda / Pratibha**: what does authority (mentor, text) and intuition say?
  - Rule: do not commit if only **one** source is feeding the decision.

- **States journal (weekly, 10 minutes)**
  - **Jagrat**: quality of waking attention this week.
  - **Svapna**: dream patterns / recurrences worth noting.
  - **Sushupti**: how was sleep recovery (subjective, plus actual hours)?
  - **Turiya glimpses**: moments of pure presence with no commentary — when, doing what, with whom?

- **Samskara starve / feed (weekly, 5 minutes)**
  - Name 1 samskara (recurring impression) you want to **starve** — what input creates it?
  - Name 1 samskara you want to **feed** — what input nourishes it?
  - Make one concrete environment change that supports the choice.

### What good execution looks like
- You catch identification ("I *am* angry") and shift to observation ("anger is present in the instrument") — multiple times a day.
- Decisions cite at least two of the three knowledge sources.
- Dreams and sleep are no longer "noise" — they are inputs you read.
- Samskara budget is intentional, not reactive.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Mind is an instrument, not the Self**: the mind is *subtle matter*; the Self is the witness consciousness. Confusing them is the root error.
- **Consciousness is primary**: behavior and biology are downstream effects, not the foundation.
- **Continuity of the "I"** across waking, dream, deep sleep, and *Turiya* implies a constant beyond moods and states — and this constant is the only stable platform for action.
- **Multiple valid epistemologies**: a fully Western-materialist stance is *limited in scope*. Inference and testimony / intuition are not optional decorations.
- **Karma operates through samskaras**: today’s impressions are tomorrow’s involuntary tendencies — the system you maintain *is* the future self you are building.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If a thought hurts you, ask: *Am I the thought or the one observing it?*
- If you’re about to commit on **one** source of knowledge, **stop** and triangulate.
- If you’re tired or "off," check Manas overload before changing strategy.
- Do not deposit a samskara today that you are not willing to *be* tomorrow.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, practice-protecting)
- **Identification slips caught / week**: moments you noticed fusion with thought / mood and shifted to witness.
- **Multi-source decisions / week**: % of significant decisions that used at least 2 of the 3 knowledge sources.
- **States journal completed / week** (yes/no — binary, no rating).
- **Samskara intentionality**: 1 starved + 1 fed each week.

### Review loop
- **Weekly (10 min)**: which Antahkarana faculty (Manas / Ahamkara / Buddhi) was loudest this week, and what input is feeding it?
- **Monthly**: one structural change — environment, schedule, inputs, company — to reduce a recurring samskara.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you are *studying* Indian psychology a lot but never stepping into the witness stance during the day, **delete the study time** and reinstate the daily 60-second witness checks first.
