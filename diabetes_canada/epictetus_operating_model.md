# Epictetus Operating Model — Diabetes Canada, *South Asian Diabetes Management Toolkit*

> Source frame: managing diabetes is **not a life sentence of restriction** — it is small, intentional choices that honor **health and heritage**. The operating stack from Diabetes Canada: (1) **portion without a scale** — the **Handy Portion Guide** (both hands = vegetables, fist = grains/fruit, palm = protein, thumb tip = fats) and the **Plate Method** (½ vegetables, ¼ protein, ¼ whole grains); (2) **available carbohydrates** — Total Carbs minus Fiber = what actually raises blood sugar; fiber-rich staples (lentils, chickpeas, whole wheat chapati) are superior buffers; (3) **strategic dining** — high-impact swaps (papad over samosa, chapati over paratha, raita over creamy dressing, fruit over mithai), sauces on the side, baked not fried, resist upsizing; (4) **timing and pairing** — meals **4–6 hours apart**; eat protein/fat *with* carbs to blunt spikes; respect the **20-minute fullness lag** (slow down); test **before meals (4–7 mmol/L)** and **2 hours after start (5–10 mmol/L)**; (5) **movement as lifestyle** — **150 minutes/week** aerobic activity plus **2–3×/week resistance**; **embrace inconvenience** (microwave balance, pace on calls, move the remote); reduce sedentary time with **3-minute breaks every 30 minutes**; (6) **SMART goals + decisional balance** — specific, measurable change tied to personal motivation. Carb targets: women **45–60g/meal**, men **60–75g/meal**, snacks **15–30g**. The unit of control is **balanced plate + timed movement + glucose data**.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Manage blood glucose while **keeping cultural foods and social dining** on the table.
- Portion accurately **without a kitchen scale** — hands and plate method at every meal.
- Count **available carbs** (total minus fiber) for stable glucose.
- Hit **target ranges** through meal spacing, pairing, and strategic restaurant choices.
- Build **150 min/week** of activity plus daily anti-sedentary habits.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Build every plate by the method**: ½ vegetables, ¼ protein, ¼ grains — or the hand guide.
- **Subtract fiber**: available carbs = total carbs − fiber when reading labels or estimating.
- **Space meals 4–6 hours apart** — avoid the glucose rollercoaster from grazing or skipping.
- **Pair carbs with protein/fat** — never eat carbohydrates alone at a meal.
- **Slow down eating** — respect the 20-minute fullness signal before taking seconds.
- **Test and use data**: before meal (4–7) and 2 hours after start (5–10 mmol/L).
- **Move daily**: 150 min/week aerobic + resistance 2–3×/week + mini movement breaks.
- **Resist upsizing** — larger portions tempt overeating regardless of "value."

### Routines / checklists

- **Plate / hand build (every meal)**
  - Vegetables: both hands full (potatoes don't count as veg here).
  - Grains/starches/fruit: fist-sized portion (basmati, brown, or parboiled rice; whole wheat roti).
  - Protein: palm-sized, little-finger thickness (chicken, fish, paneer, dahl).
  - Fats: thumb-tip only (limit ghee/oil).

- **Available carb check**
  - Read label or estimate: Total Carbohydrate − Fiber = Available Carbohydrate.
  - Stay within targets: women 45–60g/meal, men 60–75g/meal, snacks 15–30g.

- **Dining-out protocol**
  - Review menu ahead; ask baked not fried, sauces/gravies on the side.
  - Swaps: papad/upma/salad over samosa/pakora; chapati over paratha/puri; raita over creamy dressing; fruit/lassi over mithai/kheer.
  - Pack half immediately or share entrée; buffet — browse first, one pass, plate method.
  - Don't skip meals before an event (leads to overeating).

- **Glucose monitoring**
  - Test before eating (target 4–7 mmol/L).
  - Test 2 hours after meal start (target 5–10 mmol/L).
  - If high: check balance — enough protein/fat with carbs? Portion size? Timing?

- **Movement stack**
  - 150 min/week moderate aerobic (e.g., 30 min × 5 days walking).
  - Resistance 2–3×/week (weights, bands, body weight).
  - Mini breaks: balance on one leg at microwave/kettle; pace on phone calls; timer every 30 min to stand/stretch 3 min.
  - Embrace inconvenience — remote across room, stairs over elevator, park farther.

- **SMART goal setting**
  - Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-oriented (e.g., "walk 35 min/day for 2 months").
  - Use decisional balance: pros/cons of changing vs staying the same.

### What good execution looks like
- Every meal is built by plate/hand method — at home, at weddings, at restaurants.
- Available carbs are counted; fiber-rich South Asian staples are preferred.
- Glucose readings stay in target ranges most of the time.
- Meals are spaced 4–6 hours; eating is slow enough to honor fullness.
- 150 min/week activity plus anti-sedentary habits are consistent.
- Cultural dining continues with strategic swaps, not social isolation.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Portion control beats deprivation**: you keep the foods you love at the right size.
- **Fiber buffers glucose**: available carbs — not total carbs — determine the spike.
- **Pairing blunts spikes**: protein and fat slow carb absorption; carbs alone spike fast.
- **Timing stabilizes**: 4–6 hour spacing prevents rollercoaster; 20-minute lag prevents overeating.
- **Data drives adjustment**: testing reveals what *your* body does with *your* meals.
- **Movement improves insulin sensitivity**: aerobic + resistance + less sedentary time = better clearance.
- **Heritage and health coexist**: swaps and requests preserve the social meal without surrendering targets.
- **Small SMART changes compound**: decisional balance + specific goals beat vague "eat better."

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you can't weigh it, **use your hands** — they're always with you.
- If reading a label, subtract **fiber** to get available carbs.
- If dining out, **swap and request** — don't default to fried, upsized, or sauce-drowned.
- If post-meal glucose is high, check **pairing, portion, and pace** before blaming the food entirely.
- If tempted to skip a meal before a party, **don't** — it causes overeating later.
- If sedentary >30 min, **move** — microwave balance, pace, stretch.
- If a goal is vague, make it **SMART** — specific, measurable, time-bound.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading + outcome)
- **Plate/hand adherence**: meals built by method (daily count).
- **Available carb accuracy**: meals within target range (45–60 women / 60–75 men).
- **Glucose in range**: pre-meal 4–7 and 2-hr post 5–10 mmol/L (% of readings).
- **Meal spacing**: 4–6 hours between meals maintained.
- **Activity minutes**: weekly aerobic total (target 150) + resistance sessions (2–3).
- **Sedentary breaks**: mini-movement breaks per day (target: every 30 min).
- **Dining-out swaps used**: high-impact substitutions at social meals.

### Review loop
- **Per meal (when testing)**: pre and 2-hr post readings — what did this meal do?
- **Weekly**: activity minutes, plate adherence, average glucose trend.
- **Monthly**: SMART goal progress; adjust carb targets or swaps based on data.
- **Before events**: plan menu strategy (review online, pre-select swaps, don't skip prior meal).

### Core rule guardrail
- If glucose keeps spiking, the fix is usually **portion, pairing, or pace** — not abandoning your cultural foods. Return to the hand/plate method, subtract fiber for available carbs, space meals 4–6 hours, and move — because managing diabetes is small intentional choices at the table and between meals, not a life sentence of restriction or isolation.

## Operating Sequence
1. Build every plate with the hand guide or Plate Method — half vegetables, quarter protein, quarter grains.
2. Count available carbs (total − fiber); pair carbs with protein/fat; space meals 4–6 hours apart.
3. Eat slowly; at restaurants, swap strategically and resist upsizing — honor heritage with smart requests.
4. Test glucose before and 2 hours after; use readings to adjust portions and pairings.
5. Hit 150 min/week activity, resistance 2–3×/week, and mini movement breaks — set SMART goals and review weekly.
