# Comprehensive Study Guide: Diabetes Prevention and Management

This study guide synthesizes essential information regarding the management and prevention of diabetes, with a focus on dietary strategies, physical activity, and lifestyle adjustments for the South Asian community.

## Section 1: Understanding Diabetes and Nutrition

### Short-Answer Quiz

**1. What is the physiological cause of diabetes and what role does glucose play in the body?**
Diabetes occurs when the body is unable to properly use and store glucose, a form of sugar that serves as the body’s main source of energy. Glucose is derived from foods containing carbohydrates, such as fruits, milk, grains, and starchy vegetables; when the body cannot process it, sugar builds up in the bloodstream.

**2. Describe the "Plate Method" for balancing a meal.**
The Plate Method is a visual tool used to manage portions and ensure a nutritional balance at each meal. According to this model, half of the plate should be filled with vegetables, one-quarter with protein foods, and the final quarter with whole grains or starchy vegetables.

**3. How does the "Handy Portion Guide" use the human hand to estimate food portions?**
The guide suggests using the fist to represent a portion of grains, starches, or fruit, and the palm of the hand (thickness of the little finger) for protein. Vegetables should equal the amount that can be held in both hands, while added fats should be limited to the size of the tip of the thumb.

**4. What are three specific strategies for managing portion sizes when dining at a restaurant?**
When eating out, individuals can manage portions by asking the server to pack half of the meal as leftovers before it is served or by sharing an entrée with a companion. Additionally, one can request a half-portion or resist "upsizing" meals even if offered as a bargain.

**5. What are the general carbohydrate targets for men and women per meal and snack?**
For most people, women should aim for 45 to 60 grams of carbohydrates per meal, while men should aim for 60 to 75 grams. Both men and women are generally advised to limit snacks to between 15 and 30 grams of carbohydrates.

**6. When should blood glucose be tested to determine the impact of a meal?**
To understand how specific foods affect blood sugar, individuals should check their levels before eating and then again two hours after the start of the meal. The target for most people is 4 to 7 mmol/L before meals and 5 to 10 mmol/L two hours afterward.

**7. How do sugar alcohols differ from regular sugars in terms of blood glucose impact and side effects?**
Sugar alcohols, often found in "sugar-free" products, are only partially absorbed and have no major effect on blood sugar levels. However, consuming more than 10 grams a day can lead to gastrointestinal side effects such as gas, bloating, or diarrhea.

**8. What is the difference between aerobic activity and resistance exercise?**
Aerobic activity is continuous movement, such as walking or bicycling, that elevates the heart rate and breathing to improve cardiovascular fitness. Resistance exercise involves brief, repetitive movements using weights, bands, or body weight to build muscle strength.

**9. List four "Top 10" tips for reducing sedentary time during the day.**
Individuals can sit less by setting a timer for mini-movement breaks every 30 minutes, standing up to take phone calls, and choosing to use the stairs instead of an elevator. Another effective tip is to move daily-use items further away so one must stand up to retrieve them.

**10. What are the components of a SMART goal in the context of lifestyle change?**
A SMART goal must be Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, and Time-oriented. For example, instead of a vague goal to "lose weight," a SMART goal would be to "lose 5 kg in 2 months" to provide clear motivation and a way to track progress.

### Answer Key

1.  **Diabetes and Glucose:** Diabetes is the inability to use/store food for energy; glucose is the sugar from carbohydrates used for fuel.
2.  **Plate Method:** 1/2 vegetables, 1/4 protein, 1/4 grains/starches.
3.  **Handy Portion Guide:** Fist = grains/fruit; Palm = protein; Two hands = vegetables; Thumb tip = fats.
4.  **Dining Out Portions:** Pack leftovers early, share entrées, request half-portions, avoid upsizing.
5.  **Carbohydrate Targets:** Women (45-60g/meal), Men (60-75g/meal), Snacks (15-30g for both).
6.  **Testing Times:** Before the meal (4-7 mmol/L target) and 2 hours after the start (5-10 mmol/L target).
7.  **Sugar Alcohols:** Minimal blood sugar impact; excess (>10g) causes gas, bloating, or diarrhea.
8.  **Aerobic vs. Resistance:** Aerobic is continuous heart-elevating movement (walking); resistance builds muscle (weights).
9.  **Reducing Sedentary Time:** Movement timers, standing for calls, taking stairs, "embracing inconvenience" by moving items away.
10. **SMART Goals:** Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, and Time-oriented.

## Section 3: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Aerobic Activity** | Continuous exercise that makes you breathe harder and your heart beat faster, such as brisk walking, skating, or bike riding. |
| **Blood Glucose** | The amount of glucose (sugar) present in the blood at a given time. |
| **Carbohydrate** | One of the three main nutrients in food; found in grains, fruits, milk, and starchy vegetables. It is broken down into glucose for energy. |
| **Diabetes** | A condition where the body cannot properly use and store glucose for energy, leading to high blood sugar levels. |
| **Gestational Diabetes** | A form of high blood sugar that occurs specifically during pregnancy. |
| **Interval Training** | A method of exercise involving short periods of vigorous activity alternating with recovery periods or rest. |
| **Physical Activity** | Any form of movement that causes the body to burn calories, including household chores, gardening, and walking. |
| **Pre-diabetes** | A state where blood glucose levels are higher than normal but not yet high enough to be diagnosed as type 2 diabetes. |
| **Resistance Exercise** | Brief repetitive exercises using weights, bands, or body weight designed to build muscle strength. |
| **RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion)** | A 10-point scale used to identify exercise intensity based on total physical stress, effort, and fatigue. |
| **Sugar Alcohols** | Sweeteners (like sorbitol or xylitol) found in "sugar-free" foods that are only partially absorbed and have minimal impact on blood sugar. |
| **Type 1 Diabetes** | A condition where the pancreas produces no insulin, requiring insulin injections for management. |
| **Type 2 Diabetes** | A condition where the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or the body does not use it properly; managed via diet, activity, and often medication. |
