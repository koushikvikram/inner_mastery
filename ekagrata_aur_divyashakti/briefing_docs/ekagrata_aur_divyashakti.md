# The Science of Mental Concentration and Personal Magnetism

## Executive Summary

This document synthesizes the core principles and practical methodologies found in the treatise *Ekagrata aur Divya Shakti* (Concentration and Divine Power), based on the work of O Hashnu Hara. The text positions "Vichar-Vidya" (the Science of Thought) as a rigorous, rule-bound discipline rather than mere philosophy. It argues that while the roots of this science lie in ancient Indian traditions—specifically Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras—it has been refined and scientifically validated by Western "New Thought" practitioners.

The central thesis posits that organized thought is the most potent force in the world, capable of generating "Ojas" (Personal Magnetism), which facilitates success, health, and spiritual realization. By understanding the brain as a biological dynamo and the ether (Akash) as a medium for thought waves, individuals can train their mental faculties through specific exercises, including concentration intervals and simplified Pranayama.

---

## The Philosophical and Scientific Foundation

### The Re-Emergence of Thought Science

The text identifies a significant gap in the education of mental and mystical sciences. It asserts that "Vichar-Vidya" is a systematic science that requires diligent study and practice.

- **Historical Context:** The science is described as an ancient Indian discovery once mastered by sages (Munis). Over time, it was lost in India but preserved and tested in the West under the name "New Thought" (Nav-Vichar).
- **Scientific Validation:** The document emphasizes that the West has applied "acid tests" of scientific inquiry to these ancient truths, transforming them from "impossible tales" into a "True Science."
- **Objective:** The primary goal is not entertainment but the improvement of the human condition, seeking to provide tools for those burdened by poverty, ill-health, or lack of success.

### The Power of Organized Thought

A fundamental distinction is made between disorganized and organized mental activity.

- **Disorganized Thought:** Characterized as "useless" or "worthless."
- **Organized Thought:** Defined as the "greatest power in the world," more potent than money. It is argued that while money is often viewed as all-powerful, it is actually subordinate to organized thought, which can attract wealth and banish poverty.

## Biological and Mechanical Analogies of the Mind

### The Brain as a Dynamo

The text provides a detailed anatomical and mechanical framework for understanding mental power:

- **The Micro-Instrument:** The brain is described as an extremely subtle instrument or a "dynamo" (a motion-driven electrical generator). Just as a dynamo produces electricity, the brain produces thought.
- **Anatomy of Thought:**
  - **Cerebrum (Big Brain):** Identified as the seat of experience, will, intelligence, and emotions. Damage to this area results in the loss of mental powers.
  - **Cerebellum (Small Brain):** Manages involuntary muscles and protects the body’s movements.
  - **Sub-conscious Mind:** Described as a "self-proven" seat of power that functions even when the conscious mind is not directed.
- **The Nervous System:** The text outlines twelve pairs of cranial nerves that act as "wires" for the dynamo, transmitting information regarding smell, sight, taste, and motor functions to various parts of the body, including the heart, lungs, and stomach.

### Thought Transmission and the Ether (Akash)

Thought is not contained within the skull but travels through an invisible medium.

- **The Medium of Akash:** Thought waves travel through the ether (Akash) similarly to how light travels from distant stars.
- **Wave Mechanics:** Thought is described as "atomic" in nature. When a person thinks, it creates a disturbance in the ether, sending out waves.
- **Law of Action and Reaction:** Just as a ball thrown at a wall returns to the sender, thought waves sent into the universe eventually return to the thinker, carrying the "force" or "results" of the original intent.

## Ojas: Personal Magnetism and Attraction

### Defining Ojas

The term "Ojas" (also referred to as Auj, Sammohini Shakti, or Personal Magnetism) represents the physical and social manifestation of mental concentration.

- **Utility:** It is used for both spiritual development (God-realization) and material gain (wealth acquisition).
- **Characteristics:** Individuals with high Ojas possess a "magnetic attraction" that influences others and creates opportunities for success, health, and joy.

### The Mental "Magnetic Field"

The brain operates like a magnet with positive and negative poles.

- **Positive and Negative Poles:** In a mental state, a positive mind attracts while a negative mind repels.
- **Radiant Energy (Deept-bal):** This refers to the power distributed by a "body" (the brain) into its surroundings. The extent of a person's "field" depends on the strength and concentration of their mental dynamo.
- **Sympathetic Vibration:** Similar to how two magnets interact, two minds can influence each other through "Mental Union" (Telepathy), creating a combined field that exerts pressure on the ether.

## Practical Methodology for Mental Development

### Incremental Concentration Training

The text warns against attempting advanced mental feats without foundational training.

1. **Selection:** Choose a pleasing object or subject.
2. **Initial Focus:** Hold the mind on this object for one minute.
3. **Progression:** Gradually increase the duration to two minutes, then eventually to thirty minutes of continuous focus.
4. **Mental Discipline:** The goal is to move from a "disorganized, flickering mind" to a "one-pointed" focus.

### Pranayama and Visualization

A simplified method of Pranayama, attributed to the grace of Swami Satyanandaji, is provided as a core exercise.

- **The Heart Center:** Practitioners are instructed to visualize a heart symbol (labeled with the letter 'Ka') as the center of physical and mental vitality.
- **Breath and Will:** By regulating the breath and directing the will toward the heart center, the practitioner can "turn" their mental powers inward, preventing the leakage of "Ojas" through the external senses.
- **The "Love and Power" Affirmation:** Practitioners are encouraged to use specific mantras or affirmations, such as "I am Love" and "I am Power," to mold the mind and body into a desired state.

### Generating "Thought Waves" for Success

To achieve success in business or life, one must:

- **Knot the Thoughts:** Interweave strong "thought waves" around a specific goal.
- **Visual Focus:** Treat thoughts as physical objects. Just as one can see the glow of electricity around a wire, one must visualize the "glow" of their thought around their objective.
- **Consistency:** Daily practice of two to three times is required to reorganize the "brain machine" to naturally produce success-oriented vibrations.

## Ethical and Spiritual Considerations

- **Self-Responsibility:** Every individual is the architect of their own future. Current thoughts will manifest as future physical realities.
- **Universal Love:** The highest form of personal magnetism is rooted in "Love." A person who fills themselves with Love becomes a "conscious magnet" that the world cannot resist.
- **Internal Source of Power:** The document concludes that all power and progress come from within. External aids are secondary to the internal "treasure house" of the soul, which can be unlocked through the science of concentration.
