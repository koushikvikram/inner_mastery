# Study Guide: Concentration and the Acquirement of Personal Magnetism

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the principles and practices outlined in the text *"Ekagrata aur Divyashakti"* (Concentration and Divine Power), a Hindi adaptation and commentary based on the works of the philosopher O Hashnu Hara. The text synthesizes ancient Indian wisdom with Western scientific frameworks to explore the development of mental focus and personal magnetism.

## Part II: Answer Key

1. The book is intended for individuals seeking to improve their lives, gain mental power, and achieve peace, health, and success. It is not meant for simple amusement but is presented as a systematic "Thought-Science" (Vichar-Vidya) that requires diligent practice and mental effort.

2. The text suggests that Westerners call this science "New Thought," but it is actually a recovery of ancient Indian knowledge that was lost over time. It acknowledges that Western philosophers like O Hashnu Hara have systematized these ancient truths into a form that is now being reintroduced to India.

3. Unorganized thought is described as a "useless thing" that leads to failure and weakness. In contrast, organized thought is characterized as the greatest power in the world, capable of attracting wealth, overcoming poverty, and achieving significant life goals.

4. The brain is likened to a delicate machine or a "dynamo" (gati-janya-vidyuyantra) that generates thought as a form of energy. It is composed of specific parts like the cerebrum and cerebellum, which act as the physical apparatus for experience, will, and intellect.

5. Akash is an invisible, all-pervading medium (ether) through which thought waves travel, similar to how light and magnetic waves move through space. It allows thoughts to be sent across distances and to attract objects or influences toward the thinker.

6. Ojas, or Personal Magnetism, is an attractive force within a person that can be used for both spiritual development (God-realization) and material success (wealth). It is the power that creates a "magnetic" presence, making a person influential and capable of achieving their desires.

7. Pranayama is considered a vital tool for concentration because the breath is closely linked to mental stability. The text includes specific breathing methods, such as those simplified by Swami Satyanandji, to help students steady the mind and focus their internal energy.

8. The Solar Plexus is described as a center from which deep breaths originate, exerting a profound influence on mental focus and psychic powers. Exercises involving the Solar Plexus are intended to strengthen the internal "dynamo" and enhance the student's ability to project thought.

9. The law states that every thought sent out will eventually return to the sender with the same force. If one sends out thoughts of love and strength, they receive the same; however, negative or weak thoughts will also reflect back upon the thinker.

10. Induction is a scientific principle used to explain how magnetic or mental force can be transferred from one object (or mind) to another without direct contact. It describes the process by which a strong, focused mind can influence and "magnetize" its surroundings or other people.

**Instructions:** Use the themes and data points from the source context to develop detailed responses for the following prompts. *(Answers are not provided for this section).*

1. **The Mechanics of Thought:** Discuss the text's portrayal of thought as a physical and vibrational force. How does the analogy of the "dynamo" and "ether" help explain the movement of mental energy?

2. **East Meets West:** Analyze how the text reconciles ancient Indian spiritual practices (like Yoga and Pranayama) with Western scientific concepts (like Induction and Personal Magnetism).

3. **The Ethics of Influence:** Based on the sections regarding "Personal Magnetism" and "Ojas," what are the practical and moral implications of developing such a power for the individual?

4. **Concentration as a Discipline:** Evaluate the methods described for training the mind. How do visualization and rhythmic breathing contribute to the ultimate goal of Ekagrata (One-pointedness)?

5. **The Role of the Subconscious:** Explore the text's mention of the "Sub-conscious mind" and its relation to the "involuntary muscles" and the cerebellum. How does this internal regulation support the seeker's external success?

## Part IV: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Akash (Ether)** | The subtle, invisible medium that fills all space and serves as the carrier for light, magnetism, and thought waves. |
| **Cerebellum (Chota Mastishk)** | The part of the brain associated with involuntary muscles and the subconscious mind; it regulates internal bodily protections. |
| **Cerebrum (Bada Mastishk)** | The primary part of the brain responsible for experience, will, intellect, and conscious impulses. |
| **Dynamo** | A metaphor used to describe the brain's function as a generator of mental electricity or thought-force. |
| **Ekagrata** | The state of mental concentration or "one-pointedness" required to master the science of thought. |
| **Induction** | The process by which a magnetic or mental force is produced in a body by the proximity of an electrified or magnetized body without contact. |
| **Manipur-Chakra** | The Solar Plexus; a vital energy center in the body used in breathing exercises to enhance mental and physical power. |
| **Ojas** | A term for personal magnetism or the "aura" of attraction that an individual develops through concentration and health. |
| **Pranayama** | The practice of controlled breathing used as a foundational technique to achieve mental focus and spiritual advancement. |
| **Vichar-Vidya** | "Thought-Science"; the systematic study and application of mental laws to achieve success and self-improvement. |
| **Vichar-Tarang** | Thought-waves; the vibrations produced by the mind that travel through the ether to influence the external world. |
