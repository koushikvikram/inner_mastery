# Epictetus Operating Model — Dr. Ryan Martin, *Emotion Hacks: 50 Ways to Feel Better Fast*

> Source frame: emotional health is not one "big fix" or a seismic life change — it is a **Hackpack** of ~50 small, science-backed shifts used as a navigation system. The core engine is the **"Why We Feel" model**: you don't react to the stimulus, you react to your **appraisal** of it (stimulus → mood → interpretation → feeling → action tendency). Moods "poison" interpretations (Other-Directed-Shoulds, Catastrophizing), so control lives upstream. The strongest leverage is **antecedent-focused** (James Gross): Situation Selection, Situation Modification, Attentional Deployment, and Cognitive Change — acting *before* the feeling starts. Two myth-busts anchor the doctrine: **don't "vent"** and **don't use intense exercise as reactive anger management** (angry exertion is linked to heart-attack risk; exercise is a preventative Mood Hack, not Response Modulation). The master skill is **Pattern Recognition** — diagramming emotions to catch when you're reacting to the past (the "Thursday pager" ghost) rather than the present. The unit of control is your **mood, your interpretation, and your environment**.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Treat emotions as a **navigation system**, not random events that happen *to* you.
- Build and carry a **Hackpack** of small, reusable interventions instead of waiting for a "big fix."
- Intervene **upstream** (mood, interpretation, environment) before feelings escalate.
- Increase *wanted* emotions (curiosity, joy), not just suppress unwanted ones.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Don't vent reactively**: no breaking things; no intense "angry exercise" to discharge acute anger.
- **Check the appraisal**: name the interpretation before reacting to the stimulus.
- **Use exercise proactively**: as a baseline Mood Hack, not as Response Modulation mid-crisis.
- **Protect uninterrupted sleep**: target fragmentation, not just total hours.
- **Win before the feeling**: deploy antecedent strategies (select/modify situation, deploy attention).
- **Diagram recurring emotions**: run Pattern Recognition to separate present from past.

### Routines / checklists

- **Diagram the Emotion (5 parts)**
  - Stimulus → Mood → Interpretation → Feeling → Expression.
  - Ask: am I reacting to now, or to a history that's no longer relevant (the "pager" ghost)?

- **Check the Entitlement (Hack 24)**
  - When anger spikes, catch Other-Directed-Shoulds ("they *should* have…") and Catastrophizing.
  - Re-appraise: "I emote in response to my interpretation, not the stimulus."

- **Antecedent strategies (Gross) — act before the feeling**
  - Situation Selection: skip the draining gathering.
  - Situation Modification: bring a friend to the stressful event.
  - Attentional Deployment: use the "host's dog" grounding focus at the anxious party.
  - Cognitive Change: reframe the meaning before the feeling locks in.

- **Sleep-integrity hacks (break the rumination loop)**
  - Lower room temperature; set caffeine curfews; enforce blue-light boundaries an hour before bed.

- **Build the Hackpack (3 questions)**
  - What are my recurring triggers? How do I typically feel/act? What are the common themes (powerless, unrecognized)?

### What good execution looks like
- You pause at appraisal and re-interpret before reacting.
- You never "vent" through destruction or angry high-intensity exertion.
- Exercise is a steady preventative habit, decoupled from acute anger.
- Sleep is continuous; daytime rumination drops.
- You catch "ghost" patterns fast and the feeling dissipates once named.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **You react to appraisal, not reality**: identical stimuli produce opposite feelings (the B+ exam).
- **Moods poison interpretations**: an anxious baseline manufactures shoulds and catastrophes.
- **Upstream beats downstream**: antecedent-focused regulation is more effective than reactive modulation.
- **Venting compounds arousal**: discharging anger physically can raise cardiac risk, not relief.
- **Fragmented sleep drives rumination**: continuity matters more than duration.
- **Pattern Recognition is power**: most "nonsensical" feelings are the past intruding on the present.
- **Many small hacks > one big fix**: resilience is a portfolio, so you're never "out of options."

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you're acutely angry, do **not** vent or do intense exercise — re-appraise or change the situation.
- If a feeling seems irrational, diagram it and ask whether it's a ghost from the past.
- If a situation reliably drains you, select out of it or modify it in advance.
- If anxiety rises in a setting, deploy attention (the dog) instead of fighting the crowd.
- If moods feel random, audit sleep fragmentation before blaming circumstances.
- If you feel "out of options," reach into the Hackpack — the fix is a small shift, not a life overhaul.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, self-tracked)
- **Appraisal-catch rate**: how often you re-interpret before reacting.
- **Antecedent share**: proportion of regulation done *before* the feeling vs. after.
- **Vent avoidance**: zero destructive/angry-exercise discharges in acute states.
- **Sleep continuity**: nighttime wake-ups trending down.
- **Pattern hits**: recurring triggers identified and named.
- **Wanted-emotion frequency**: curiosity/joy intentionally increased.

### Review loop
- **In the moment**: which of mood, interpretation, or environment can I hack right now?
- **Daily**: diagram one strong emotion — stimulus, mood, interpretation, feeling, expression.
- **Weekly**: review recurring triggers and common themes; add/refine Hackpack tools.
- **Monthly**: are sleep continuity and antecedent share improving? Which ghosts have faded?

### Core rule guardrail
- If feelings stay overwhelming, you're managing **downstream** — move upstream to mood, interpretation, and environment, and remember you emote in response to your appraisal, not the stimulus. Never use venting or angry exertion as the release valve; reach for the Hackpack instead.

---

## Operating Sequence
1. When a strong emotion hits, pause and check the appraisal before reacting (no venting).
2. Diagram it — stimulus, mood, interpretation, feeling, expression — and test for a past "ghost."
3. Apply an antecedent hack: select or modify the situation, or deploy attention.
4. Protect the baseline: proactive exercise and uninterrupted sleep to keep the system resilient.
5. Review recurring patterns weekly and grow the Hackpack so you're never "out of options."
