# Epictetus Operating Model — Epictetus, *The Enchiridion*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Epictetus, *The Enchiridion*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: the whole art of living turns on one cut — the **dichotomy of control**. "Some things are in our power and others are not." In our power: opinion, desire, aversion, impulse — *prohairesis*, the faculty of choice. Not in our power: body, property, reputation, office, and every outcome. Suffering comes not from events but from our **judgments** about them ("Men are disturbed not by the things which happen, but by the opinions about the things"). Therefore: confine **desire and aversion** to what is yours, treat externals as borrowed and "given back," play your assigned **roles** well, rehearse hardship in advance (**premeditation of evils**), and on every appearance test "is this mine or not mine?" before assenting. Freedom is wanting reality to be as it is.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Locate the line between **what is up to me** (judgment, desire, aversion, impulse) and **what is not** (everything external) — and live entirely on my side of it.
- Want events "to happen as they do happen" so that life flows freely.
- Keep *prohairesis* (the will/choice) unconquered, since nothing external can force it without my assent.
- Discharge each **role** (son, citizen, worker, friend) excellently, leaving the casting to "the playwright."

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Run the dichotomy on every disturbance**: "Is this in my power or not?" If not, say "it is nothing to me" and withdraw desire from it.
- **Withdraw aversion from the unavoidable**: never set aversion against externals (death, loss, others' conduct) — only against misuses of your own choice.
- **Add the reserve clause** to every plan: "I will do X, *if nothing prevents*." The aim was to act well, not to control the result.
- **Pause on the appearance**: between impression and assent, say "wait for me a little, let me see what you are."
- **Demand nothing from the world; demand everything of yourself** — locate the fault inside, where the remedy is.

### Routines / checklists

- **Morning premeditation (premeditatio malorum, 2–3 min)**
  - Name what the day may take: delay, criticism, illness, a broken plan, a difficult person.
  - Rehearse: "This can happen, and it is external — it cannot touch my choice."
  - Set one aim *within* your power for the day (act justly, keep my word, stay even).

- **The control sort (whenever upset, 30 sec)**
  - State the thing plainly.
  - Sort it: *mine* (judgment/desire/aversion/action) or *not mine* (outcome/others/body/reputation).
  - If not mine: "it is nothing to me" — redirect attention to my response.
  - If mine: this is where I act now.

- **Appearance test (on every strong impression)**
  - Greet it: "You are an appearance, not at all the thing you claim to be."
  - Ask: what judgment am I about to assent to?
  - Strip the addition: the event is bare; the "terrible" is my opinion added on.
  - "It seemed so to him" — when others wrong you, the offense is your judgment, not their act.

- **Role review (assigned-part check)**
  - Name the role in play (father, employee, neighbor, citizen).
  - Ask what *that role* requires here — duty flows from relationship.
  - Play it well; "it is another's part to assign the role."

- **The handle rule (for grievances)**
  - Find the handle by which the matter *can* be carried: "my brother is unjust" (won't carry) vs. "my brother, my kin" (carries).
  - Take up the bearable handle.

- **"Give it back" practice (for attachment/loss)**
  - Of anything you love, say "I have given it back," not "I have lost it" — child, cup, position were always lent.
  - Use the cup-and-child rule: love what is mortal as mortal, and you will not be shattered when it breaks.

### What good execution looks like
- Disturbance triggers the dichotomy automatically, not a spiral of complaint.
- Desire is reserved for what is wholly within your power; you are no longer hostage to externals.
- Plans carry the reserve clause, so a thwarted outcome does not register as a personal defeat.
- You blame neither others nor yourself in raw events — you examine the *judgment*.
- You can state, for the current situation, your role and its duty.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **The dichotomy of control is the root**: confound the two columns and you get hindrance, grief, and blame; keep them clean and no one can compel or harm you.
- **It is not events but judgments**: the disturbing power lives in opinion; remove the opinion and the "harm" dissolves.
- **The will (*prohairesis*) is invincible**: externals can chain the leg but never the choice unless it consents.
- **Externals are "given back," not lost**: ownership is a loan; treating it so removes the sting of loss.
- **Role ethics**: duties are "measured by relations" — what to do is read off who you are to whom.
- **Freedom is alignment with nature**: "Seek not that things happen as you wish, but wish them to happen as they do."
- **Progress is shown by conduct, not talk**: the philosopher's work is borne out in deeds, especially under pressure.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If desire or aversion attaches to an external → cut it; reserve desire for choice alone.
- If you feel wronged → the wound is your opinion; "if you think yourself harmed, you can decide not to be."
- If you start a plan → append "if nothing prevents," and judge yourself only on acting well.
- If you don't yet have the strength to face a thing → don't parade as a philosopher about it; act, stay quiet, let deeds speak.
- If someone reports you were insulted → answer the *judgment*, not the report: "had he known the rest, he'd have mentioned more."

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, choice-tied)
- **Dichotomy catches / day** (disturbances correctly sorted into mine vs. not-mine).
- **Reserve-clause plans / week** (intentions framed as "act well, if nothing prevents").
- **Assent delays** (instances where you paused the impression before assenting).
- **External-desire incidents** (target: trending to 0 — desire placed on outcomes you don't control).
- **Blame redirections** (grievances turned from "they/the world" back to "my judgment").

### Review loop
- **Daily (evening, 3 min)**: Where was I disturbed? Which column did it belong in? Did I assent to a false appearance? What role did I owe, and did I play it?
- **Weekly**: Which external am I still secretly demanding? Re-rehearse it as "not mine."
- **Monthly**: Are my deeds, not my words, showing progress — calmer under loss, steadier under insult, freer in plans?

### Core rule guardrail
- If you find yourself *talking about* Epictetus while still grieving over externals and blaming the world, you have moved the doctrine into the not-in-your-power column. Sheep don't bring grass to the shepherd to show how much they ate — they show it as wool. Stop quoting; run the dichotomy on the very thing in front of you, now.

---

## Operating Sequence

1. **Premeditate**: name the day's possible losses as externals before they arrive.
2. **Sort**: on every disturbance, run the dichotomy of control — mine or not mine.
3. **Test the appearance**: pause; refuse assent to the added judgment; keep the event bare.
4. **Reserve desire and aversion**: want only what is up to you; release the rest as "given back."
5. **Play the role**: act the duty your relationships assign, and act it well.
6. **Review by deeds**: each evening, judge progress by conduct under pressure, not by words.
