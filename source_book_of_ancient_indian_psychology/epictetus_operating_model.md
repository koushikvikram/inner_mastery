# Epictetus Operating Model — Source Book of Ancient Indian Psychology


![Epictetus Operating Model — Source Book of Ancient Indian Psychology](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame (Kuppuswamy, drawing from Upanishads, Samkhya-Yoga, Buddhism, Jainism, Charaka): *Atmanam Viddhi* — know the Self. The chariot model is the operating diagram: **Atman** (the lord) → **Buddhi** (charioteer / intellect) → **Manas** (reins / mind) → **Indriyas** (horses / senses) → **Body** (chariot). The two pedals are **Abhyasa** (steady practice) and **Vairagya** (detachment). Five **Kleshas** (afflictions) bend the wheel. Three **Gunas** (Sattva / Rajas / Tamas) are the chemistry of personality, in flux. The precondition for everything else is **Chittasthairya** — mental stability.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- **Right hierarchy**: Atman in command, Buddhi steering, Manas obedient, Indriyas trained — not the inverse.
- **Chittasthairya**: a stable mind capable of clarity, ethics, and skill.
- **Pair Abhyasa + Vairagya**: practice without detachment becomes obsession; detachment without practice becomes lethargy.
- **Reduce Klesha load**: identify the loudest affliction and weaken its inputs.
- **Cultivate a Sattvic recipe**: shift the Guna mix toward clarity by changing inputs, not by force of will.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Daily Abhyasa + Vairagya pair**: one repeated practice forward, one deliberate non-engagement with a habitual pull.
- **Chariot check-in**: notice who is currently driving and reset the hierarchy.
- **Klesha audit (weekly)**: name the loudest of the five and weaken one of its inputs.
- **Guna recipe check (weekly)**: estimate the mix; reduce one Tamas input, channel one Rajas input toward focused work.
- **Antahkarana hygiene (daily)**: brief end-of-day pass over Manas, Ahamkara, Buddhi.

### Routines / checklists

- **Morning chariot check-in (2 minutes)**
  - Who is currently driving — Buddhi (intellect), Manas (mind), or Indriyas (senses)?
  - Reset hierarchy: *Atman → Buddhi → Manas → Indriyas → Body.*
  - Set today’s one Buddhi-led intent.

- **Daily Abhyasa + Vairagya pair (must be done together)**
  - **Abhyasa**: one repeated practice toward your aim (focus block, training rep, study, craft).
  - **Vairagya**: one deliberate non-engagement with a known pull (food, scroll, validation, drama).
  - Mark both. They are *paired* — neither alone is the practice.

- **End-of-day Antahkarana hygiene (5–7 minutes)**
  - **Manas**: any unprocessed sensory load to discharge?
  - **Ahamkara**: where did I take something personally that wasn’t personal?
  - **Buddhi**: which decision was clean (deliberation), and which was autopilot?

- **Weekly Klesha audit (10 minutes)** — pick the loudest of the five
  - **Avidya** (ignorance): treated something impermanent as permanent?
  - **Asmita** (egoism): identified self with a role / title / instrument?
  - **Raga** (attachment): chased pleasure compulsively?
  - **Dvesha** (aversion): avoided discomfort compulsively?
  - **Abhinivesa** (clinging): acted from fear of loss / death / status-end?
  - Pick **one input** that feeds the loudest klesha and reduce it next week.

- **Weekly Guna recipe check (5 minutes)** — rough %
  - **Sattva** (clear / calm / luminous)
  - **Rajas** (driven / agitated / restless)
  - **Tamas** (dull / heavy / inert)
  - Reduce **one Tamas input** (dull food, doomscroll, oversleep, clutter).
  - Channel **one Rajas input** into focused work (deadline → deep work block).

- **Therapeutics (when out of balance — Charaka’s three modes)**
  - **Divine**: ritual, contemplation, time in nature, silence.
  - **Scientific**: sleep, diet, exercise, medication.
  - **Mental control**: restrain mind from unwholesome objects (environments, content, company).
  - Use *all three*, not one in isolation.

### What good execution looks like
- The chariot is in order most of the day; when it inverts (senses dragging the chariot), you notice and reset.
- Abhyasa and Vairagya are *paired* — every day. No imbalance.
- You can name this week’s loudest klesha and the one input you’re weakening.
- Your guna recipe is shifting toward Sattva *via inputs*, not via white-knuckling.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Suffering = identification with the impermanent**: *Atmanam Viddhi* is the corrective.
- **Mind is an instrument**: the Antahkarana (Manas + Ahamkara + Buddhi) is a tool of the Self, not the Self.
- **Personality is a recipe in flux**: gunas change with inputs — you are not "fixed."
- **Chittasthairya is the precondition**: ethics, skill, and clarity are downstream of mental stability — there are no shortcuts past it.
- **Two pedals only**: practice without detachment becomes obsession; detachment without practice becomes inertia. Both, paired.
- **Kleshas operate as defaults**: they don’t need encouragement; they only need *unblocked inputs*. Your job is to block one input at a time.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If today had no Abhyasa — *or* no Vairagya — the day didn’t happen, regardless of output.
- When stuck, ask: *Who is currently driving the chariot?* Reset hierarchy before changing strategy.
- Treat your week’s loudest klesha as your *only* lever this week. Don’t scatter.
- Change inputs before changing intentions.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, practice-protecting)
- **Abhyasa days / Vairagya days** — they should match.
- **Chittasthairya minutes / week** (silent, stable attention; not "felt productive" — *stable*).
- **Klesha-of-the-week** named, with one input reduced.
- **Guna recipe** rough % (weekly self-estimate); 4-week trend.
- **Antahkarana hygiene completed / week**.

### Review loop
- **Weekly (15 min)**:
  - Which klesha was loudest, and which input fed it?
  - Was the Abhyasa-Vairagya pair intact every day?
  - Where did the chariot invert (senses dragging buddhi)?
- **Monthly**: change one input that structurally feeds your loudest klesha — environment, schedule, company, content.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you can name all five kleshas, all three gunas, and all three sources of knowledge — but skipped your **daily Abhyasa + Vairagya pair** — delete the dashboard and re-anchor on the daily pair.
