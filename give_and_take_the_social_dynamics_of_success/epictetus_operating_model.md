# Epictetus Operating Model — Adam Grant, *Give and Take*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Adam Grant, *Give and Take*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: success is shaped by **reciprocity style**. The world contains **Takers** (get more than they give), **Matchers** (tit-for-tat), and **Givers** (other-focused). At the top *and* the bottom of any field you find Givers — but only one type makes it to the top: the **Otherish** Giver, who has high concern for others *and* high concern for self. The mechanics are: the **Five-Minute Favor**, the strength of **Dormant Ties**, **Powerless Communication** (vulnerability, asking, advice-seeking) that earns **Prestige** rather than Dominance, **Expedition Behavior** (group mission over personal credit), and protection against **Takers in Givers’ clothing** through **Sincerity Screening**. Avoid the burnout trap by **chunking** your giving and tying it to **visible impact**.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Operate as an Otherish Giver — high other-interest *and* high self-interest.
- Build a network of Five-Minute Favors and Dormant Ties.
- Use Powerless Communication to gain Prestige (not Dominance).
- Protect yourself from Takers without becoming one.
- Avoid Giver burnout by chunking + impact visibility.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Run the Five-Minute Favor protocol** weekly.
- **Reactivate one Dormant Tie** per month.
- **Use powerless communication** in influence moments (vulnerability, asking, advice-seeking).
- **Sincerity-screen** any "Giver" who looks helpful — verify with the *little people* test.
- **Chunk your giving** instead of sprinkling, to preserve energy.
- **Distribute credit honestly**: practice Expedition Behavior, beware Responsibility Bias.

### Routines / checklists

- **Five-Minute Favor (weekly)**
  - Identify 1–3 favors that take ≤ 5 minutes for you and create disproportionate value for someone.
  - Examples: an intro, a recommendation, a piece of feedback, a resource share.
  - Do them. No expectation of return. No follow-up bookkeeping.

- **Dormant Tie reactivation (monthly)**
  - List people you haven’t spoken to in 1+ year who once mattered.
  - Pick one. Reach out with a Five-Minute Favor or genuine update — *not* a transactional ask.
  - Outcome over time: novel information channels reopen.

- **Powerless Communication drill (in influence moments)**
  - Replace assertions with **questions** ("What do you think?").
  - Replace certainty with **vulnerability** (admit a mistake / a not-knowing).
  - Practice **advice-seeking** — it builds prestige *and* gives you better information.
  - Drop the puffery; let the substance lead.

- **Sincerity Screening (before trusting a "Giver")**
  - Watch how they treat **someone who can do them no good** (Samuel Johnson test).
  - Watch their CEO-photo equivalent (do they hog credit?).
  - Test small before trusting big.
  - Match Takers in negotiation; don’t fold.

- **Anti-burnout chunking (weekly)**
  - Bundle giving into 1–2 dedicated blocks, not 20 micro-interruptions.
  - Bundle around **visible impact** (you can see the help land).
  - Cap weekly giving budget; protect the rest for deep work.

- **Expedition Behavior pass (after every shared win)**
  - List the upstream contributors and dependencies.
  - Distribute credit publicly.
  - Audit your own Responsibility Bias: what felt like "mine" was probably 50% theirs.

### What good execution looks like
- You can name 3 Five-Minute Favors you did this week.
- You reactivated one Dormant Tie this month — without an ask attached.
- In a recent influence moment you led with a question, not an assertion.
- You can describe a Taker you sincerity-screened and adjusted to.
- Your giving is chunked and energizing, not scattered and draining.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Givers occupy both top and bottom**: the difference is *Otherish vs. Selfless*. Self-interest is not the enemy.
- **The marathon belongs to Givers**: in interconnected, service-driven work, reservoirs of goodwill compound.
- **Dormant Ties carry novel information**: strong ties recycle what you already know.
- **Vulnerability builds prestige**; dominance builds resentment.
- **Matchers police Takers**: Takers eventually fall because most people enforce justice (Ultimatum Game).
- **Responsibility Bias destroys collaborations**: you over-credit your own contribution; the cure is deliberate attribution.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you’re overwhelmed by giving, you’re probably *sprinkling*. Chunk it.
- If you’re negotiating with a Taker, **match** — don’t default to giving.
- If your influence is low, switch from assertion to question and admit one not-knowing.
- If a "Giver" is described as charming above and rough below, sincerity-screen and protect yourself.
- If a credit conversation is happening, distribute first, claim second.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Five-Minute Favors / week**.
- **Dormant Ties reactivated / month**.
- **Powerless-comm moments / week** (questions over assertions; vulnerability shown).
- **Sincerity screens completed / quarter** (and changes made afterward).
- **Giving load distribution** (% chunked vs. sprinkled).
- **Credit-distribution behaviors / week**.

### Review loop
- **Weekly**:
  - Did I help anyone with no expected return?
  - Where did I default to dominance instead of prestige?
  - Where am I over-giving and burning out?
- **Monthly**: review your "Solar System" — who’s a Taker, Matcher, Giver in your life right now?
- **Quarterly**: re-evaluate which Givers you want to invest more in.

### Core rule guardrail
- If your generosity is making you brittle (resentful, exhausted, overlooked), you’ve drifted from Otherish to Selfless. Restore self-interest first — chunk, set boundaries, match Takers — *then* keep giving.
