# Epictetus Operating Model — Carol Dweck, *Mindset*

> Source frame: every domain (sports, business, relationships, education) splits between the **Fixed Mindset** (qualities are carved in stone; effort means low ability; failure = identity verdict) and the **Growth Mindset** (qualities can be cultivated; effort is the path; failure = information). Praising **intelligence** creates fragility; praising **process, effort, and strategy** creates growth. The "**naturals**" are usually the hardworkers — Edison had 30 assistants; Mozart labored 10+ years; MJ was cut from his high school varsity. The most powerful word in the model is **"yet"**: not "I can’t" but "I can’t *yet*."

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Operate from Growth Mindset by default; catch and reframe Fixed Mindset voice in real time.
- Praise (self and others) on **process, effort, strategy** — never on raw ability.
- Convert failure from a verdict into information.
- Keep stretching at the edge — refuse "running in place" to protect an image.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Treat current ability as the slope, not the line.**
- **Use "yet"** whenever a Fixed Mindset sentence appears.
- **Praise process over product** (yours and others’).
- **Choose the harder task** when the choice is between "look smart" and "learn."
- **Treat criticism as information**, not as a hit on identity.

### Routines / checklists

- **Morning Mindset reset (2 min)**
  - Read once: *"My current ability is the slope, not the line. I am here to stretch, not to prove."*
  - Identify today’s edge — the thing you don’t yet know how to do.
  - Commit to one rep at that edge.

- **The "Yet" drill (in real time)**
  - Whenever a Fixed sentence appears, append "yet":
    - *"I can’t do this"* → *"I can’t do this **yet**."*
    - *"I’m not a [identity]"* → *"I’m not a [identity] **yet**."*
  - Notice the shift from verdict to trajectory.

- **Process-based praise drill (every interaction)**
  - When praising others (or self):
    - **Bad**: "You’re so smart / talented / a natural."
    - **Good**: "You found a great strategy. Your process worked."
    - **Best**: "What did you try? What didn’t work? What did you learn?"

- **Stretch-task selection (weekly)**
  - List the week’s tasks.
  - Pick the one that risks looking un-smart but teaches the most.
  - Schedule it first, when energy is high.
  - Outcome: deliberately invest in growth over image.

- **High-effort reframe (when the "talent excuse" tempts you)**
  - "If I had to work this hard, I’m not a natural" → reframe:
  - "If I work this hard, I become great. The naturals worked harder than this."
  - Edison had 30 assistants. Mozart labored 10 years. MJ was cut. Effort *is* the talent.

- **Failure-as-information protocol (after any setback)**
  - Resist the verdict ("I am a failure" / "I’m slime").
  - Convert into information:
    - What specifically went wrong?
    - What did the failure reveal that I didn’t know?
    - What is the next experiment?
  - Action becomes a fact, not an identity.

- **Champion’s mindset under pressure**
  - You don’t need to feel perfect to perform.
  - On bad days, ask: *what is one strategy I can still execute well?*
  - "Heartbreaks" become fuel, not exits.

### What good execution looks like
- "Yet" appears in your inner monologue without effort.
- You can describe today’s stretch task and what you learned from it.
- Praise (yours and to others) describes process, not nature.
- Failure produces a written experiment, not a hidden shame.
- You catch yourself "running in place" within hours — and pivot to stretching.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Mindset is a belief, beliefs are changeable**: the framework is not a personality test, it’s a choice.
- **Praising intelligence creates fragility**: ~40% of ability-praised students lied about scores; identity protection drives dishonesty.
- **Effort is the path, not a sign of weakness**: the Fixed view of effort is what blocks the natural from becoming great.
- **The myth of the lone natural is an exit**: it lets us avoid the toil that growth requires.
- **Failure ≠ identity**: action is a fact, not a verdict on the soul.
- **Champions perform when the conditions aren’t right**: mindset, not mood, is the differentiator.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If a sentence carves a quality in stone, append "yet" or rewrite it.
- If you’re about to praise ability, redirect to praising process.
- If a task feels safe, you’re probably running in place — choose the stretch.
- If a setback looks like an identity verdict, convert it into one written experiment.
- If you’re hiding effort to look like a natural, you’ve confused image with growth.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **"Yet" reframes / week** (catches in real time).
- **Stretch tasks chosen / week** (over safer alternatives).
- **Process-praise events / week** (replacing ability-praise).
- **Failure-as-experiment notes / month** (written, not buried).
- **Hidden-effort detections / month** (where you tried to look like a natural).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: did I stretch or did I run in place?
- **Weekly**: where did the Fixed voice show up — and did I catch it?
- **Monthly**: list the *yets* you turned into *doings* this month.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re *teaching* Growth Mindset but secretly performing for "look-smart," you’ve flipped the model. Pick one stretch task this week that risks looking un-smart, and do it publicly. Without that, the framework is a slogan, not a practice.
