# Epictetus Operating Model — James Clear, *Atomic Habits*

> Source frame: outcomes are a **lagging measure of habits**. Goals are interchangeable; **systems** decide who actually wins. Change is non-linear — most people quit in the **Valley of Disappointment** before crossing the **Plateau of Latent Potential**. The deepest layer is **identity**: every action is a vote for the person you are becoming. The mechanics are the **Habit Loop** (Cue → Craving → Response → Reward) governed by **Four Laws of Behavior Change**: Make it Obvious, Make it Attractive, Make it Easy, Make it Satisfying (with inversions to break bad habits). Compound interest of 1% beats heroic action; design the environment, then trust the system.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Become the person whose default behavior produces the desired outcome.
- Run on systems, not motivation.
- Survive the Valley of Disappointment by trusting the math of compounding.
- Engineer the environment so the right behavior is the path of least resistance.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Cast a vote daily** for the identity you’re becoming (the goal is to *become a runner*, not "run a marathon").
- **Apply the Four Laws** to every habit you want to install.
- **Apply the inversions** to every habit you want to break.
- **Use the Two-Minute Rule** to start any habit you keep skipping.
- **Track the system, not the outcome** for the first 30 days.

### Routines / checklists

- **Identity-first morning anchor (2–3 min)**
  - Write or say: *"Today I am [identity], and a [identity] does X."*
  - Pick the *one* tiny behavior that casts that vote today.
  - Begin with that behavior — small, irreducible.

- **Habit Stacking template (anchor new behaviors)**
  - Use: *"After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."*
  - Examples (rotate):
    - After I pour coffee, I meditate for 1 minute.
    - After I sit down to dinner, I name one gratitude.
    - After I get into bed, I plan tomorrow’s first action.

- **Four Laws design pass (every new habit, 5–10 min)**
  - **Obvious**: visible cue (place, time, environment) defined.
  - **Attractive**: paired with something you already crave (temptation bundle).
  - **Easy**: scaled to ≤ 2 minutes for the entry version.
  - **Satisfying**: built-in immediate reward + tracker tick.

- **Four Laws inversion pass (any habit you want to break)**
  - **Invisible**: remove the cue from your environment.
  - **Unattractive**: re-frame the cost (what you lose).
  - **Difficult**: add friction (commitment device, distance, lock).
  - **Unsatisfying**: introduce an immediate consequence (accountability partner / cost).

- **Two-Minute Rule (whenever resistance hits)**
  - Don’t do the full habit; do the gateway version.
  - "Read for an hour" → "open the book."
  - "Run 5 km" → "lace up shoes."
  - Show up first; expand later.

- **Plateau crossing protocol (when nothing seems to work)**
  - Re-read the math: 1.01^365 ≈ 38; 0.99^365 ≈ 0.03.
  - Remind yourself: results are *stored*, not wasted.
  - Continue 2 more weeks before changing the system.

- **Nightly habit-tracker tick (60 sec)**
  - Mark the chain.
  - Never miss twice.
  - If you missed today, plan tomorrow’s first action.

### What good execution looks like
- Identity statements are concrete and recently used.
- Each new habit has a stack anchor and a Two-Minute version.
- The environment shows the system (visible cues for good habits, hidden cues for bad).
- "Never miss twice" is enforced — slips happen, drift doesn’t.
- You can name what you’re voting for this week, in identity terms.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Goals are interchangeable; systems differentiate**: every Olympian wants gold — only some have the system.
- **You don’t rise to your goals; you fall to your systems**: in pressure moments, the floor is your habits.
- **Identity is the deepest layer**: outcome → process → identity. Lasting change is identity change.
- **Aggregation of marginal gains beats heroic action**: tiny 1% improvements compound across many surfaces.
- **Environment beats willpower**: design the cue and the friction; willpower is a finite resource.
- **The Plateau is real**: most growth is invisible until a phase change.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you can’t find motivation, scale the habit down to 2 minutes — don’t reach for "willpower."
- If a habit isn’t sticking, run the Four Laws audit; one of the four is broken.
- If a bad habit keeps recurring, run the inversions on the same loop.
- If results aren’t showing, evaluate the *system*, not the goal.
- If you missed a day, do not miss tomorrow.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Identity vote count / week** (concrete instances of acting as the desired identity).
- **Habit consistency rate** (per habit, weekly).
- **Two-day-miss incidents** (target: 0).
- **Friction edits / month** (environmental changes made for or against habits).
- **System changes / month** (intentional, not reactive).

### Review loop
- **Weekly (10 min)**:
  - Which habits cleared 90% adherence?
  - Where did the Four Laws break?
  - What environmental friction needs adjusting?
- **Monthly**: identity check — am I closer to the *person*, not just the *result*?

### Core rule guardrail
- If you can’t name the identity you’re voting for, you’re back in goal-chasing. Pause the tracker, restate the identity, then resume. Without identity, habits decay back to defaults.
