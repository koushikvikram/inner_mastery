# Epictetus Operating Model — Goleman, Boyatzis & McKee, *Primal Leadership*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Goleman, Boyatzis & McKee, *Primal Leadership*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: the **primal task** of leadership is emotional — to prime good feeling and drive a group's collective mood toward optimism. This works because the **limbic system is an open-loop system**: through **interpersonal limbic regulation**, **mirroring**, and **emotional contagion**, the leader becomes the strongest "seasoning" in the group's emotional soup. The leader's job is to create **resonance** (a reservoir of positivity that frees the best in people) and avoid **dissonance** (the "flooding" of an **amygdala hijack** that the **prefrontal cortex** failed to veto). The engine is **Emotional Intelligence** across four domains — Self-Awareness, Self-Management, Social Awareness, Relationship Management — deployed through a **repertoire of six styles** (Visionary, Coaching, Affiliative, Democratic, Pacesetting, Commanding). The "soft" climate is a hard asset: a 1% improvement in service climate predicts a 2% revenue increase, and climate drives 20–30% of business performance.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Manage your own emotional state first, so you can manage the group's.
- Generate resonance: a climate where people feel understood, in synch, and free to do their best work.
- Match the leadership style to the moment instead of defaulting to one club.
- Make EI competencies operational, not decorative — tie them to retention, climate, and revenue.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Seed the mood deliberately**: decide what emotional "seasoning" you are adding before you walk into the room.
- **Veto the hijack**: when threatened, let the prefrontal cortex intercept the amygdala before you speak or send.
- **Lead with empathy**: attune to the group's actual emotional reality before driving toward yours.
- **Select the style, don't impose it**: name the situation, then choose the style that fits.
- **Use humor as connection**: laughter is a Limbic Lock and a trust signal — never force it.
- **Refuse the SOB shortcut**: never buy short-term results by burning human capital.

### Routines / checklists

- **Self-Management pre-brief (2–3 min before any high-stakes interaction)**
  - Name your current emotion (Self-Awareness gate).
  - Ask: is this resonance or dissonance I'm about to transmit?
  - Reset breathing/posture so the PFC is in control, not the amygdala.

- **Resonance read (start of every meeting)**
  - Scan the room: what is the group actually feeling right now?
  - Attune and name it (à la Loehr on 9/11 — "manage meaning" before tasks).
  - Only then move the mood toward the work.

- **Style-selection pass (per situation)**
  - Need a new direction / shared dream → **Visionary**.
  - Developing a person's long-term capability → **Coaching**.
  - Healing rifts / stress → **Affiliative**.
  - Need buy-in / input from capable people → **Democratic**.
  - Motivated, competent team needing speed → **Pacesetting** (short bursts only).
  - Genuine crisis / turnaround → **Commanding** (attack the problem, not the person).

- **Amygdala-hijack recovery protocol (when flooded)**
  - Recognize the surge (heart rate up 20–30 bpm = no clear thinking possible).
  - Pause; do not deliver inept criticism while either party is flooded.
  - Re-engage only after the prefrontal-limbic circuitry is back online.

- **EI competency cadence (weekly)**
  - Self-Awareness: one honest review of where your mood drove the room.
  - Self-Management: one impulse you successfully vetoed.
  - Social Awareness: one moment you read an unspoken current correctly.
  - Relationship Management: one act of inspiration, influence, or developing others.

### What good execution looks like
- People take their emotional cues from you and trust them.
- You can name which of the six styles you used today and why.
- Dissonant styles (Pacesetting, Commanding) are rare, bounded, and free of contempt.
- Criticism is delivered without triggering hijacks; talent stays.
- Laughter is frequent and genuine — a sign the open loop is healthy.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Leadership is primal and emotional first**: the *how* (emotional tone) determines whether the *what* (strategy) works at all.
- **The limbic system is open-loop**: you cannot opt out of emotional contagion — you only choose its direction.
- **Resonance frees performance; dissonance withers it**: a flooded brain cannot hear or respond clearly.
- **Self-awareness is the foundation**: the other three EI domains cannot stand without it.
- **Styles are clubs, not identities**: the best leaders carry a full bag and switch fluidly.
- **Empathy is a hard asset**: tuned-out leaders are the top reason talented people quit.
- **Abrasive success is steroid abuse**: coercion inflates short-term numbers while hollowing out the organization.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you feel the surge, veto before you speak — never lead from a hijack.
- If the room's mood is off, attune and name it before pushing the agenda.
- If you're using one style for everything, you've stopped leading and started defaulting.
- If you must use Commanding or Pacesetting, time-box it and strip out contempt.
- If a "win" requires burning people, it's a loss on a delay.
- If talent is leaving, audit your empathy before your strategy.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, climate-first)
- **Service/team climate score** (the leading indicator of revenue — 1% up ≈ 2% revenue up).
- **Style repertoire breadth** (number of styles used appropriately per month).
- **Hijack incidents** (times you led from a flooded state — target: declining).
- **Resonance signals** (genuine laughter frequency, voluntary candor, discretionary effort).
- **Talent retention of high performers** (the empathy ledger).
- **EI competency reps** (instances logged per domain weekly).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: one-minute mood debrief — what seasoning did I add to the soup today?
- **Weekly**: style audit (did I match style to situation?) + one EI competency to strengthen.
- **Monthly**: climate check among direct reports; resonance vs. dissonance trend.
- **Quarterly**: do my "soft" wins show up as hard outcomes (retention, climate, growth)?

### Core rule guardrail
- If results look good but the climate is curdling and talent is eyeing the door, you are running on dissonant fuel. Stop chasing the score, restore self-management and empathy, and rebuild resonance — the numbers are a lagging echo of the mood.

---

## Operating Sequence

1. Regulate yourself first (Self-Awareness → Self-Management).
2. Attune to the group's real emotional state (Social Awareness).
3. Choose the style that fits the situation, not your habit.
4. Drive the mood toward resonance and lead the work.
5. Review climate and style fit; strengthen one EI competency and repeat.
