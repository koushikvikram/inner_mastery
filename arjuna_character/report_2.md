# Comprehensive Study Guide: The Life and Character of Arjuna

This study guide provides an exhaustive review of the character and adventures of Arjuna as presented in the provided source material. It explores his training, his relationship with the Divine, his military triumphs, and the philosophical crises that defined his journey as the central hero of the *Mahabharata*.

---

## Part I: Review Quiz

**Instructions:** Answer the following questions in 2-3 sentences based on the information provided in the sources.

**1. What is the significance of the names "Arjuna," "Dhananjaya," and "Savyasachi"?**
Arjuna means "spotless" or one of spotless fame who glows like silver. Dhananjaya refers to his ability to conquer over enemies and bring back immense wealth, particularly for the performance of sacrifices. Savyasachi denotes that he is ambidextrous, capable of fighting and shooting arrows with equal expertise using both arms.

**2. How did Arjuna demonstrate his dedication to archery during his time at the gurukul?**
After a gust of wind extinguished a lantern while he was eating, Arjuna realized he could find his mouth in the dark by instinct and concluded he could also shoot arrows in the dark. He began practicing tirelessly at night, eventually mastering the skill of using sound to detect and accurately hit invisible targets.

**3. Why did Arjuna accept a one-year exile despite King Yudhishthira’s willingness to waive the punishment?**
Arjuna chose exile to honor a strictly established rule regarding the brothers' privacy with Draupadi, which he had violated to retrieve his weapons and help a distressed Brahmin. He insisted that truth must be followed without compromise or "impersonalism," refusing to use his elder brother's permission as an excuse to avoid the consequences of his actions.

**4. Describe the weapons and equipment Arjuna received from Agni and Varuna for the burning of the Khandava Forest.**
Arjuna was gifted the Gandiva, a celestial bow as tall as a man that created a thunderous sound when twanged. He also received two inexhaustible quivers of arrows and a magnificent jeweled chariot featuring a standard with the emblem of Hanuman.

**5. How did Arjuna defeat the Nivatakavachas and Kalakeyas in the celestial realms?**
Despite the demons using mystical illusions like floods, falling rocks, and total darkness, Arjuna used celestial weapons and his training in shooting invisible targets to counter their magic. Ultimately, he invoked the *Pashupatastra*—a weapon of Lord Shiva—which manifested as thousands of missiles and predatory beasts to pulverize the demonic races.

**6. What role did Arjuna play in the kingdom of Virata during the final year of the Pandavas' exile?**
Arjuna lived incognito as Brihannala, a eunuch who taught dance and music to the king’s daughter, Uttara. During the Kuru cattle raid, he revealed his prowess by single-handedly defeating the entire Kuru army, including Bhishma, Drona, and Karna, to protect King Virata’s honor and wealth.

**7. Why did Arjuna choose Krishna as a non-combatant advisor over the powerful Narayani Sena?**
Arjuna’s choice was rooted in his deep devotion and friendship, believing that the presence and guidance of Krishna were more valuable than any military force. He recognized Krishna as the source of all power and intelligence, famously stating that where there is Krishna, there is certain victory.

**8. What were the primary arguments Arjuna raised in the Bhagavad Gita against participating in the Kurukshetra War?**
Arjuna argued based on compassion for his relatives, the lack of enjoyment he would feel in a kingdom won through bloodshed, and the fear of sinful reactions. He also worried about the social disruption and moral degradation of the community that would follow the death of so many family protectors.

**9. How was the death of Jayadratha achieved despite his father’s protective benediction?**
To fulfill Arjuna’s vow before sunset, Krishna used his Sudarshana Chakra to temporarily hide the sun, tricking Jayadratha into coming out of hiding. Arjuna then shot a specialized arrow that carried Jayadratha’s head into the lap of his father, Vridhakshatra, ensuring the head did not touch the ground and cause Arjuna's own death.

**10. What occurred during Arjuna’s encounter with his son Babruvahana in Manipur?**
During the Ashvamedha Yajna, Babruvahana fought his father to prove his kshatriya prowess, eventually striking Arjuna with a fatal celestial arrow. Arjuna was later revived by a mystical jewel provided by the Naga princess Ulupi, who explained the event was a penance for Arjuna's role in the unfair death of Bhishma.

## Part II: Answer Key

1. **Significance of Names:** Arjuna (spotless/silver), Dhananjaya (conqueror of wealth), Savyasachi (ambidextrous).
2. **Gurukul Dedication:** Realized he could eat in the dark; practiced archery at night using sound detection.
3. **Reason for Exile:** Followed the principle of truth; refused to waive the penalty for interrupting Yudhishthira and Draupadi.
4. **Khandava Equipment:** Gandiva bow, two inexhaustible quivers, and the Hanuman-standard chariot.
5. **Celestial Victories:** Countered illusions with celestial knowledge; used the *Pashupatastra* to destroy the Nivatakavachas and Kalakeyas.
6. **Virata Role:** Lived as Brihannala (dance teacher); defeated the Kuru army solo during the cattle raid.
7. **Choosing Krishna:** Preferred divine guidance and friendship over brute force; recognized Krishna’s supremacy.
8. **Gita Arguments:** Compassion, loss of enjoyment, sinful reactions, and fear of social/family collapse.
9. **Jayadratha’s Death:** Krishna’s fake sunset; decapitation arrow delivered the head to the father’s lap to avoid a curse.
10. **Babruvahana:** Father and son fought; Arjuna died from a celestial arrow but was revived by Ulupi’s jewel.

## Part III: Essay Questions

**1. The Evolution of Archery Excellence:** Analyze how Arjuna’s mastery of archery is portrayed not just as a natural talent, but as a result of tireless dedication, spiritual practice, and divine favor. Use examples from his training in the dark to his acquisition of celestial weapons.

**2. Principles vs. Contextual Ethics:** Compare Arjuna’s rigid adherence to the truth during his exile for breaking privacy with his decision to follow Krishna’s guidance in the war (such as the death of Jayadratha or the verbal "killing" of Yudhishthira). How does the text define a "man of principles"?

**3. The Complexity of Rivalry:** Examine the ongoing scorecard between Arjuna and Karna. How does the narrative use their various encounters (the martial exhibition, the Swayamvar, the Gandharva fight, and the Virata war) to establish Arjuna's eventual superiority?

**4. The Hero’s Crisis:** Detail the five psychological and moral arguments Arjuna presents in the first chapters of the *Bhagavad Gita*. How does Krishna’s revelation of the soul's immortality and the nature of duty specifically address these concerns?

**5. Strategic Alliances through Character:** Discuss how Arjuna’s personal virtues—such as his sense of duty, his restraint, and his martial prowess—led to the formation of critical alliances with the Yadus, the Nagas, and the residents of Manipur.

## Part IV: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anjalika** | The specific celestial weapon used by Arjuna to decapitate Karna. |
| **Ashvamedha Yajna** | A horse sacrifice performed by a king to establish imperial sovereignty and global peace. |
| **Atirathi** | A classification of a warrior capable of fighting thousands of opponents simultaneously. |
| **Bhagavad Gita** | The "Song of God"; the transcendental conversation between Krishna and Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. |
| **Brahman** | The priestly class; Arjuna disguised himself as a Brahman to win Draupadi’s hand. |
| **Brihannala** | Arjuna’s incognito identity in the kingdom of Virata, where he acted as a eunuch dance teacher. |
| **Dharma** | Duty, virtue, or the moral order of the universe; the guiding principle of Arjuna's life. |
| **Gandiva** | Arjuna’s celestial bow, gifted by Varuna via Agni, known for its incredible strength and sound. |
| **Khandava** | The forest burned by Agni with the help of Krishna and Arjuna to cure Agni's indigestion. |
| **Kshatriya** | The warrior and administrative class to which Arjuna belonged. |
| **Maharathi** | A great warrior; the text notes Arjuna was an "Ati-maharathi," a Peerless Warrior. |
| **Maya Sabha** | The magnificent and illusory assembly hall built for the Pandavas by the architect Maya in gratitude to Arjuna. |
| **Narayana Sastra** | A formidable celestial weapon; also refers to the eternal duo of Nara (Arjuna) and Narayana (Krishna). |
| **Pashupatastra** | The fearsome weapon of Lord Shiva, capable of destroying celestials, which Arjuna acquired through austerity. |
| **Swayamvar** | A ceremony where a bride chooses her husband from a group of suitors, often involving a test of skill. |
| **Yoga Maya** | The divine internal potency of the Lord that can cover even great devotees like Arjuna for the purpose of divine pastimes. |
