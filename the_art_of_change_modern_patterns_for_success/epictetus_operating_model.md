# Epictetus Operating Model — Erik Schön, *The Art of Change* (modern *I Ching*)


![Epictetus Operating Model — Erik Schön, *The Art of Change* (modern *I Ching*)](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: *Yì* means both **change and ease** — change is not a wall, it is **water**, and progress follows the **line of least resistance**. Set **directions, not destinations** (alignment-for-autonomy in a shifting climate). **Wúwéi** is not passivity; it is "doing useful things and intervening wisely" — you hoe the earth around the plant, you do not pull on its leaves. **Shízhōng** is **timely balance** — doing exactly what fits the context at the right time; standing still is an illusion. Integrity (***Dé***) is "inner potency and wholeness" — the substance that endures when image fades. Final test: when the work is done, the community says, *"We did it ourselves."*

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Treat change as water, not as a wall — find the line of least resistance.
- Lead with **direction**, not destination — preserve autonomy in flux.
- Practice **Wúwéi**: create conditions, remove constraints, intervene only where it matters.
- Practice **Shízhōng**: act exactly to fit the moment; refuse stillness as a strategy.
- Build **Dé** (integrity, inner potency) — the substance that survives when image fails.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Map the flow before pushing**: where is the water already moving?
- **Define a direction**, not a destination, for any month-or-longer effort.
- **Hoe, don’t pull**: clear constraints, then let the work grow.
- **Run a Shízhōng read** before every major intervention.
- **Audit Image vs. Substance** weekly — keep them aligned.

### Routines / checklists

- **Water-not-wall scan (start of any change effort, 10 min)**
  - Where is energy already flowing in this system?
  - Where is the natural channel?
  - Force-fit interventions = bruising the wall; flow-with interventions = riding the current.

- **Direction-not-destination framing (monthly, for each effort)**
  - Replace: *"By Q3 we will land at [exact destination]."*
  - With: *"We are moving in the direction of [quality, capability, posture]; we will adjust the destination as the climate evolves."*
  - Outcome: alignment without rigidity.

- **Wúwéi intervention test (before any push)**
  - Are you pulling on a leaf, or hoeing the soil?
  - List the *constraints to remove* before the *actions to add*.
  - Intervene at the smallest leverage point that produces the largest natural movement.

- **Shízhōng (timely balance) read (before any major decision)**
  - Read the moment: conflict, collaboration, or retreat?
  - Read the cycle: expansion or contraction phase?
  - Read the **shì** (momentum): is the trigger about to release?
  - Choose the action that fits the moment — not your default style.

- **Image vs. Substance audit (weekly, 10 min)**
  - List what you’ve been **saying / signaling** (Image).
  - List what you’ve actually **done / built** (Substance).
  - Where they diverge: tighten the substance, not the image.

- **Tiger protocol (when accompanying power)**
  - "Accompanying a king is like accompanying a tiger."
  - Walk with eyes straight ahead — quiet confidence, no flattery.
  - Lean on substance; don’t buy safety with image.

- **"We did it ourselves" check (after a successful effort)**
  - Did the team feel the win as theirs?
  - If yes — Wúwéi worked.
  - If they were waiting for you to credit them, you over-led.

### What good execution looks like
- Major efforts are framed as directions, not destinations — yet still felt as concrete by the team.
- Interventions remove constraints first; only then do they add force.
- Big calls land in the right *moment*, not just on schedule.
- Image and Substance are not in dissonance.
- After your wins, the team narrates the victory in their own voice.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Yì = change + ease**: when correctly aligned, change is the easier path, not the harder one.
- **Water benefits all without contending**: yielding is a strategy, not a defeat.
- **Destinations arrest movement; directions enable it.**
- **Coercion provokes resistance**: pulling on the plant kills it.
- **Standing still is an illusion in flux**: motionlessness is misalignment in disguise.
- **Substance outlasts image**: in the long run, *Dé* is the only durable advantage.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you’re forcing, you’re probably misaligned with the flow — re-read the landscape.
- If your goal is a destination in a turbulent climate, convert it to a direction.
- If you’re tempted to add a control, ask first what constraint to remove instead.
- If image and substance are diverging, fix substance — never paper it with image.
- If your team can’t say "we did it ourselves," re-examine your hand on the plant.

---

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Direction statements / month** (replacing destination-only goals).
- **Constraints removed : forces added ratio** (Wúwéi posture).
- **Shízhōng reads / month** (timing reads done before major moves).
- **Image–Substance alignment score** (weekly self-rating, 1–5).
- **"We did it ourselves" testimonials / quarter**.

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: where did I push when I should have flowed?
- **Monthly**: do my directions still fit the climate? Or am I dragging an obsolete destination?
- **Quarterly**: which constraints, removed, would unlock the most natural movement?

### Core rule guardrail
- If you find yourself pulling harder on the plant, stop. Wúwéi is not laziness — it is the discipline to *not* intervene where intervention destroys. Re-read the landscape before adding force.
