# System Diagram of Achieving Consistency

![System Diagram of Achieving Consistency](./system_diagram.png)

**Attention + Honest Examination** — Pause, look directly at your current behavior, and name what is actually happening without excuses.

**Reflection** — Ask: “What is this showing me about my priorities, patterns, and choices?”

**Clarity** — Decide what matters most right now and separate it from what is merely urgent, comfortable, or distracting.

**Clear Priorities** — Write down the few things that deserve your time, energy, and protection.

**Understanding Cause & Effect** — Identify what conditions produce your desired behavior and what conditions sabotage it.

**Intentional Design** — Shape your environment, calendar, tools, constraints, and routines to make the right action easier.

**Supporting Structures** — Build practical supports: reminders, checklists, recurring blocks, accountability, defaults, limits, and feedback loops.

**Chosen Importance + Accepted Cost** — Choose what matters and consciously accept what it will require you to give up.

**Commitment** — Make a specific decision: “I will keep choosing this even when it is inconvenient.”

**Discipline** — Practice acting according to the decision instead of according to mood, impulse, or comfort.

**Repeated Aligned Action** — Take the next action that matches your priorities, then repeat it regularly.

**Practice + Emotional Regulation** — Train the behavior in small reps and learn to continue even when bored, tired, anxious, or tempted.

**Feedback** — Review what happened, measure whether your actions matched your priorities, and adjust the system.

**Consistency** — Let repeated aligned action, supported by structure and priority, become your normal pattern.

**Honest Prioritization** — Admit what is truly first, what is second, and what must be delayed, reduced, or removed.

**Accepted Cost Over Time** — Keep paying the necessary cost repeatedly until the choice becomes stable rather than occasional.
