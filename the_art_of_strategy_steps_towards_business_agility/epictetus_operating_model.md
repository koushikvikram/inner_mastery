# Epictetus Operating Model — Erik Schön, *The Art of Strategy* (Sun Tzu × Boyd × Wardley)


![Epictetus Operating Model — Erik Schön, *The Art of Strategy* (Sun Tzu × Boyd × Wardley)](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: in VUCA, planning is a liability — strategy is **architecting**. Past success is a trap (**Inertia** — Blockbuster could not abandon late fees). Innovation is a **Snowmobile**: *analysis* (deconstruct) + *synthesis* (recombine) across unrelated domains. Stop planning, start **mapping** (Wardley) — anchor on **User Need**; track evolution from **Genesis → Custom Built → Product → Commodity / Utility**; respect the **climatic patterns** the rules of supply and demand impose. Inside the OODA loop, the **second O — Orientation** — is the real engine; the goal is to *disorient* the opponent, not just out-pace them. The skill of skills (Sun Tzu) is **winning without fighting**: disrupt strategy, then alliances, then engage; copying competitors is the wasteful "attack on cities." Build via **Aptitude × Attitude**: **Explorers, Villagers, Town Planners** — match the human culture to the evolutionary stage.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Replace plans with **maps**.
- Hunt **inertia** — the pre-existing model that will eventually kill you.
- Build **snowmobiles** — synthesize across domains for novel capability.
- Win on **Orientation**, not just speed.
- Engage hierarchically: disrupt strategy → disrupt alliances → engage → never just copy.
- Match **Aptitude × Attitude** to evolutionary stage.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Map the landscape** before deciding strategy.
- **Audit inertia quarterly**: what past success is now blocking the next move?
- **Run a snowmobile session monthly**: cross-domain synthesis.
- **Strengthen Orientation** continually (heritage, tradition, experience, doctrine — but updated).
- **Refuse to copy as primary strategy** — copying is signal of lost orientation.

### Routines / checklists

- **Wardley Map (per major effort, refreshed quarterly)**
  - Anchor: explicit **User Need** at the top.
  - Value chain underneath, components placed by evolutionary stage:
    - **Genesis** (uncharted) → **Custom Built** → **Product (+rental)** → **Commodity (+utility)**.
  - Plot the climatic patterns acting on each component.
  - Decide gameplays based on movement, not on plans.

- **Inertia hunt (quarterly)**
  - List the past wins that *defined* this organization / function.
  - For each, ask: *what is the model behind this win, and where is it now obsolete?*
  - Identify the "late-fees" model you’re defending.
  - Plan an explicit divestment.

- **Snowmobile workshop (monthly)**
  - Pick 4 unrelated domains.
  - Deconstruct each into mechanisms.
  - Recombine the parts: skis + outboard + handlebars + tank treads.
  - Identify one synthesis worth a small experiment this month.

- **OODA Orientation drill (weekly)**
  - **Observe**: gather fresh data (don’t reuse last week’s reads).
  - **Orient**: cross-reference — *projections, empathies, correlations, rejections*.
  - **Decide**: pick the gameplay (don’t over-debate).
  - **Act**: ship; restart the loop.
  - Track: how many loops did we close this week vs. how many got stuck in deliberation?

- **Disorientation play (in competitive moments)**
  - Where is the competitor’s mental model rigid?
  - Make a move that doesn’t fit their map.
  - Force them to fold back into themselves.
  - Speed without surprise is a footrace; surprise wins on terrain.

- **Engagement hierarchy check (before any move against a competitor)**
  - **Disrupt strategy** — can you change the game itself?
  - **Disrupt alliances** — can you isolate them from their support?
  - **Engage** — only if odds are overwhelming.
  - **Copying** — only as deliberate temporary tactic, never as strategy.

- **Aptitude × Attitude staffing pass (per initiative)**
  - **Genesis** → put **Explorers** (high-novelty, high-failure tolerance).
  - **Custom Built / Product** → put **Villagers** (turn prototypes into trusted products).
  - **Commodity / Utility** → put **Town Planners** (efficiency, scale, deviation removal).
  - Mismatched aptitude = predictable failure; correct first.

### What good execution looks like
- Decisions are made looking at a map, not a Gantt.
- The team can name the inertia they are working against right now.
- Cross-domain insights are flowing into your bets.
- OODA loops are closing, not stalling on Orientation.
- Engagements escalate hierarchically; copying is rare and intentional.
- People are matched to the evolutionary stage of their work.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Static plans die in motion**: only maps + climatic patterns survive contact.
- **Past success is the seed of future failure**: inertia is the silent killer.
- **Novelty comes from synthesis**: new capability is a snowmobile, not an invention.
- **Orientation > speed**: the OODA edge is updated mental models, not faster cycling.
- **Winning without fighting** preserves capital and momentum for the long war.
- **Aptitude is contextual**: Explorers, Villagers, Town Planners each fail outside their stage.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you don’t have a map, you don’t have a strategy.
- If your "strategy" is copying a competitor, it’s an admission of lost orientation.
- If a past win is sacred, suspect it as inertia.
- If you’re losing on speed, you may actually be losing on Orientation.
- If you’re forcing Town Planners to do Genesis work (or vice versa), you’re paying for a mismatch every day.

---

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Maps maintained / quarter** (anchored on user need, refreshed).
- **Inertia exits / year** (past models deliberately divested).
- **Snowmobile experiments / quarter** (cross-domain syntheses tested).
- **OODA loops closed / week** (vs. stalled).
- **Disruption-strategy moves / quarter** (vs. direct engagements / copies).
- **Aptitude × Attitude alignment** (% of initiatives correctly staffed).

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: where did Orientation lag, where did action precede understanding?
- **Quarterly**: refresh maps, hunt inertia, audit aptitude × attitude.
- **Annually**: what is your Blockbuster moment — is it scheduled or sneaking up?

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re running plans without maps, you’re executing fast in the wrong direction. Map before deciding; the map *is* the strategy. Without it, you’re back to fragility dressed as discipline.
