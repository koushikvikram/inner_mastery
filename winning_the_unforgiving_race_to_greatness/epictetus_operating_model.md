# Epictetus Operating Model — Tim Grover, *Winning*

> Source frame: **Winning is uncivilized** — hard, nasty, unpolished, unforgiving, unapologetic. Motivation is a **sugar high**; the elite are already kicking their own ass. The formula is **MIND > FEELINGS**: master the scale of anger (Calm → Annoyed → Mad → Pissed → Angry → Enraged) and operate in **Controlled Rage** — calm and aggressive simultaneously. **Balance is a lie** for those who want to win — invoke the **IDGAF Muscle** to delete distractions and disappoint the right people. Be **Different** — operate at the **.0001% edge** (training on game day, steak before tip-off, biceps for intimidation). Greatness cycles **Paradise → Hell → Paradise**; survival in hell is built on the **Four Rings of Winning** — Talent (outer), Intelligence, Competitiveness, **Resilience (center)**. The pieces don’t pick themselves up — *they* pick you up, and you come back stronger.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Operate from MIND > FEELINGS — feelings inform; the mind decides.
- Channel anger into **Controlled Rage**, never into rage.
- Use the **IDGAF muscle** to delete distractions and obligations that don’t serve the win.
- Find and exploit the **.0001% edge** — small differences that compound at elite levels.
- Survive the **Paradise → Hell → Paradise** cycle by anchoring on **Resilience**.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Mind decides, feelings inform.**
- **Compress to Controlled Rage**; never let it escalate to rage.
- **Press Delete** on what doesn’t serve the mission — even when it’s polite to keep.
- **Hunt the microscopic edge** — assume 10% better isn’t enough.
- **Treat hell as scheduled** — it’s how Resilience is built.

### Routines / checklists

- **Mind > Feelings discipline (every decision)**
  - State the feeling. Acknowledge it.
  - Ask: *what would the prepared mind decide here?*
  - Act on the mind, not on the wave.
  - Feelings are weather; mind is the captain.

- **Anger scale check (in moments of provocation)**
  - Locate yourself: Calm → Annoyed → Mad → Pissed → Angry → Enraged.
  - **Enraged = loss of control = loss.**
  - Compress upward emotion into **Controlled Rage**: high-velocity aggression, surgical precision.
  - Strike with it; don’t lash with it.

- **IDGAF muscle (weekly hygiene)**
  - List the people, requests, and noise you’ve been carrying out of obligation.
  - For each, decide: serve the win, or press Delete.
  - Practice disappointing the right people on schedule — the wrong people will always be disappointed.

- **.0001% edge hunt (monthly)**
  - For your craft, list the unconventional moves the average never tries.
  - Examples for inspiration:
    - Train on game day to keep body chemistry consistent.
    - Eat the steak the conventional wisdom forbids — because the pasta starves you by Q4.
    - Add the "intimidation" rep — the visible signal that the game is over before tip-off.
  - Pick one. Run it for a month. Measure.

- **Hell-cycle protocol (when you crash from a peak)**
  - Recognize: you reached Paradise; Hell is the bill.
  - Don’t pick up the pieces — let the pieces pick you up. Let them decide how you’re reassembled.
  - Refuse to be the same when you come back. The hell is the architecture of the next gear.

- **Four Rings audit (quarterly)**
  - **Talent** (entry fee — present? sufficient?).
  - **Intelligence** (deep understanding of craft — improving?).
  - **Competitiveness** (willingness to fight for the result — sharp?).
  - **Resilience (CENTER)** — the only ring that survives hell.
  - Spend 80% of development time on Resilience, the bullseye.

- **No-balance check (when you’re tempted to "balance")**
  - Scale of Zero: equal weight on both sides = zero movement.
  - Pick the dominant side this season; let the other side wait.
  - If you aren’t willing to disappoint someone, you aren’t chasing greatness.

### What good execution looks like
- Decisions in pressure are mind-led, not feeling-led.
- Anger never explodes — it’s contained, aimed, and effective.
- Your week shows visible Deletes; nothing was kept "to be polite."
- You can name a .0001% move you’re running right now.
- After the last hell, you came back rougher and more resilient — and you can describe how.
- The Four Rings show Resilience growing fastest.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Motivation is a sugar high**: it leaves you face-planted at the first hard moment.
- **MIND > FEELINGS**: the prepared mind acts; feelings observe.
- **Controlled Rage is a Lamborghini**: high-velocity aggression with surgical precision; rage is a crash.
- **Balance is a lie at the top** — total obsession is the price.
- **Different is the secret weapon**: at elite levels, you fight for microscopic edges, not 10%.
- **Hell is curriculum** — Resilience is scar tissue of the soul; it can only be earned in fire.
- **Done = Next**: Winning escorts you out of Paradise the moment you arrive.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you need a pep talk, you weren’t ready.
- If your anger is exploding, compress it back to blue-hot or hold the strike.
- If you’re kept by obligation, press Delete.
- If your edge is "10% better," you’re still amateur — find the .0001% move.
- If you’re basking, you’re slipping — name the next goal *now*.
- If you’re asking for balance, you’re asking for zero movement.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Mind-led decisions / week** (vs. feeling-led).
- **Controlled-Rage events / month** (compressed and converted to action).
- **Deletes / week** (IDGAF muscle exercised).
- **.0001% experiments / quarter**.
- **Resilience growth signal** (post-hell capability you didn’t have before).
- **Empty-glass speed** (hours from win → next goal named).

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: where did feelings drive the call I should have made with the mind?
- **Monthly**: which Delete did I avoid? Which edge did I run?
- **Quarterly**: did Resilience grow more than Talent or Intelligence? It should.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re *talking* uncivilized but living comfortable, the model is decoration. Pick one Delete this week — the one that costs you something to make. The bill for *not* deciding is always larger than the bill for deciding.
