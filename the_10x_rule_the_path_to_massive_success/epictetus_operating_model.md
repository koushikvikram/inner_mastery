# Epictetus Operating Model — Grant Cardone, *The 10X Rule*

> Source frame: most people fail before they begin because they make a **mathematical error** — they wildly underestimate the action and resources required, then call it "realistic." 10X is two things, **not one**: **10X levels of thinking** *and* **10X quantities of action**. The single biggest problem is **obscurity** — domination is the only antidote. Success is not a choice; it is a **duty, obligation, and responsibility** — *"hungry-dog-on-the-back-of-a-meat-truck"* mentality. There are only **Four Degrees of Action**: **Do Nothing → Retreat → Normal → Massive**; **average is a failing formula** that collapses on every market contraction. Operate from the **Source mindset** (extreme ownership) — refuse the four marks of a victim. Goals are written **twice daily, in present tense, 10X what feels reasonable**. You know you’re taking enough action when you’re **creating new problems** and being labeled a workaholic. Strategy: **Expand, never contract** — Howard Schultz visited stores at 9 PM during the recession. Success is like **a breath of air** — last one was important; next one is more so.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Stop underestimating effort; price every goal at 10X what feels needed.
- Operate at the **Fourth Degree (Massive Action)** as the natural state.
- Frame success as an **ethical obligation**, not a wish.
- Be the **Source** — own everything, including what others would call uncontrollable.
- **Expand, never contract** — especially when the market is contracting.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Set 10X targets**, then 10X the *action* required.
- **Write goals twice daily**, present tense, audacious.
- **Default to Fourth Degree action**; Normal is the new failure.
- **Refuse victim-thinking** — assume responsibility for everything.
- **Embrace the labels** ("obsessive," "workaholic") as signals you’ve broken out of mediocrity.

### Routines / checklists

- **Twice-daily goals (morning + night, 5 min each)**
  - Write 5–10 goals.
  - **Present tense**: *"I own 5,000 apartments,"* not *"I will own."*
  - 10X the level your "realistic" mind tries to write.
  - It is safer to fall short of a massive goal than to hit a small one and still be broke.

- **Underestimation drill (per project)**
  - Estimate the effort, money, time, and resistance you think it will require.
  - **Multiply each by 10**.
  - Provision against *that* number.
  - Outcome: enough fuel to weather the storm of resistance and noise.

- **Four Degrees of Action audit (weekly)**
  - For the past week’s major activities, label each:
    - 1) Do nothing (energy spent justifying inaction).
    - 2) Retreat (action taken in reverse, fear-driven).
    - 3) Normal (average effort, average outcome).
    - 4) Massive (Fourth Degree).
  - Count the time at each level. The goal is more time at 4, less at 1–3.

- **Source / Extreme Ownership protocol (daily, in real time)**
  - When something goes wrong, *before* the explanation forms — ask:
    - *Where am I the Source of this?*
    - *What backup, system, or move would have prevented it?*
  - Refuse the four victim signs:
    - 1) Bad things happen to me.
    - 2) They happen often.
    - 3) I’m always involved.
    - 4) Someone / something else is to blame.
  - Translate every "they did X" into "I allowed / failed to prevent X."

- **Massive Action threshold check (weekly)**
  - Are you **creating new problems** with your volume?
    - Capacity strain, support needed, things breaking that didn’t before.
  - Are people calling you *obsessive, workaholic, intense*?
  - If neither — your action level is still inside Normal. Increase.

- **Expand-never-contract play (in any contraction / setback)**
  - Default response of the average: cut, hide, retreat.
  - 10X response: go *into* the contraction. Visit your stores at 9 PM. Show up where competitors have stopped showing up.
  - Pick one expansion move when others are contracting; execute the same week.

- **Omnipresence protocol (monthly)**
  - For your market: are you visible everywhere it matters?
  - Audit: search results, channels, communities, formats.
  - Pick the next channel of presence; ship in 30 days.

- **Overcommit + Overdeliver (per major commitment)**
  - Commit at a level that forces growth.
  - Deliver beyond the commitment.
  - This is how reputation compounds; under-promising is just a slow form of mediocrity.

- **Embrace fear as indicator (in moments of hesitation)**
  - Fear ≈ the right direction. The bigger the resistance, the bigger the move.
  - Reframe the question from *"Should I do this?"* to *"How fast can I move on this?"*

- **Breath-of-Accomplishment check (daily, end of day)**
  - Ask: *what breath of accomplishment did I take today?*
  - The last one was important — the next one matters more.
  - One forward act, every single day, no exception.

### What good execution looks like
- Goal lists are written morning and night, 10X what feels safe, present tense.
- Project provisioning is generous because you assumed 10X the effort.
- Your week shows mostly Fourth-Degree time; "Normal" hours are flagged.
- Your default response to setbacks is *Source* language, not *victim* language.
- People are starting to call you obsessive — and you’re smiling at it.
- During contractions you expanded; during quiet markets you became more visible.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Most failure is mathematical**: under-provisioned effort can’t survive resistance.
- **Obscurity is the real enemy**: the average person isn’t fighting competition — they’re losing to invisibility.
- **Success is an ethical issue**: you owe it to your family, your future, and your potential.
- **Average is a failing formula**: it survives only stable conditions; reality isn’t stable.
- **The Source mindset converts excuses into solutions**: total ownership creates total agency.
- **Massive Action is the natural state** — children operate this way until they’re trained out of it.
- **Criticism is a leading indicator of breakthrough**: the labels arrive *before* the results do.
- **Expand-when-others-contract** is asymmetric advantage at near-zero cost.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If a goal is "realistic," it’s underestimated — 10X it.
- If your action level isn’t producing *new problems*, it’s not Massive yet.
- If you’re explaining instead of owning, you’re in victim mode.
- If you’re feeling fear, the move is probably correct — accelerate.
- If the market is contracting, expand.
- If you’re "comfortable," obscurity is winning.
- If you can’t name today’s breath of accomplishment, you’re living off yesterday’s.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Goal writes / day** (morning + night present-tense list).
- **Action-level distribution / week** (% time at Degrees 1–4; target: shift toward 4).
- **New problems created / week** (proxy for Massive Action volume).
- **Source-language ratio** (Source statements vs. victim statements in self-talk and journaling).
- **Expansion moves during contractions / quarter**.
- **Omnipresence channels active**.
- **Daily breath-of-accomplishment** (1/day, non-negotiable).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: morning + evening goals; end-of-day breath check.
- **Weekly**: Four Degrees audit; new-problems count.
- **Monthly**: omnipresence audit; expansion pipeline.
- **Quarterly**: 10X-the-goal review — re-baseline ambition upward.

### Core rule guardrail
- If your week shows mostly *Normal Action* and you’re writing "10X" goals on top of it, you’re running a small engine with a giant logo. The volume of action is the proof — not the goal sheet. Increase actions until you’re creating new problems, *then* keep going. Without Massive Action, the rest of the rule is theatre.
