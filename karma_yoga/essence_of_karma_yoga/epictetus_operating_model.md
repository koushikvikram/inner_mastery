# Epictetus Operating Model — Swami Chinmayananda, *The Essence of Karma Yoga (Gita 3.30)*

> Source frame: the essence is distilled in one instruction Krishna gives Arjuna — *"Mayi sarvani karmani sannyasya... nirashir nirmamo bhutva yudhyasva vigata-jwarah"*: **surrendering all actions unto Me (the Self), with the mind resting in the Self (*adhyatma-chetasa*), free from desire/expectation (*nirashih*) and free from "mine"-ness/ego (*nirmama*), fight — free from mental fever (*vigata-jwara*).** Karma Yoga is not renouncing work but renouncing the *agitation* in the worker. Three preparations — dedicate every action to the higher (sannyasa), anchor the mind in the Self, and drop desire and ego — produce one result: **action without fever**. The unit of control is your inner attitude *while* acting; do your duty, "fight," but keep the mind cool, unburdened, and unowned.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Act in the world fully ("fight") while carrying **zero mental fever** (*vigata-jwara*) — no anxiety, no burning, no agitation.
- **Dedicate all actions** to the Self/Higher (*mayi sarvani karmani sannyasya*) so the ego is not the doer.
- Keep the **mind resting in the Self** (*adhyatma-chetasa*) rather than tossed by outcomes.
- Be **free of expectation** (*nirashih*) and **free of "mine"** (*nirmama*).

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Dedicate before acting**: offer the action to the Higher so it is not an ego-transaction.
- **Anchor the mind**: keep attention rooted in the Self, not absorbed in the result.
- **Drop desire and "mine"**: act without the expectation of fruit and without the ego's ownership of it.
- **Then act decisively**: do your duty — "fight" — wholeheartedly; detachment is inner, not withdrawal.
- **Measure by the fever, not the deed**: the test of yoga is whether the mind stayed cool.

### Routines / checklists

- **The three preparations (before any significant action, 1–2 min)**
  - **Sannyasa**: "This action is offered to the Higher; I am not its sole owner."
  - **Adhyatma-chetasa**: settle attention in the witnessing Self — one breath, mind centered.
  - **Nirashih + nirmama**: name and release the expected fruit and the "this is mine."

- **Vigata-jwara scan (during work)**
  - Notice the "fever": worry, heat, restlessness, gripping.
  - Trace it to its cause — usually desire (*asha*) or ego (*mamata*).
  - Reapply the preparation that's missing; resume acting.

- **"Fight" check (when tempted to withdraw)**
  - Detachment ≠ inaction. Is your duty calling?
  - Engage it fully; keep the renunciation in the *mind*, not in the hands.

- **Offering close (end of day)**
  - Return the day's actions to the Higher: "done, offered, not mine to claim."
  - Note where fever crept in and which preparation lapsed.

### What good execution looks like
- You act energetically and your mind stays cool — effort high, fever low.
- Before tasks you can name the offering, the anchor, and the released expectation.
- Disturbance is read as a *symptom of desire or ego*, then dissolved at its root.
- You never use "surrender" as a cover for avoiding duty.
- The mark of progress is steadiness of mind, not the size of the outcome.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **The fever is the enemy, not the work**: Karma Yoga removes agitation from the actor, not action from life.
- **Dedication dissolves the ego-doer**: when all actions are surrendered to the Higher, there is no anxious "I" to burn.
- **Mind in the Self is unshakable**: anchored in *adhyatma*, the mind is not whipsawed by fruits.
- **Desire and "mine" are the fuel of fever**: *nirashih* (no expectation) and *nirmama* (no ownership) cut off the supply.
- **Right inner attitude makes any action yoga**: the same deed binds with fever and liberates without it.
- **Act, don't flee**: "yudhyasva" — engage the battle of duty; renunciation is internal.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If the mind is feverish → don't change the task first; restore dedication, anchor, and desirelessness.
- If you crave the result → apply *nirashih*; offer the fruit and resume.
- If you feel "this is mine / I did this" → apply *nirmama*; return the action to the Higher.
- If you want to withdraw to "stay peaceful" → check it against duty; engage and keep the peace inside.
- If you can't locate the fever's cause → it's desire or ego; release whichever is gripping.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, attitude-tied)
- **Fever-free actions / day** (significant actions performed with a cool mind).
- **Preparation completeness** (share of actions begun with all three: sannyasa, adhyatma, nirashih/nirmama).
- **Fever catches / week** (agitations noticed and traced to desire or ego).
- **Ownership returns** (instances of releasing "mine"/credit on a completed action).
- **Avoidance-as-detachment incidents** (target → 0: duties dodged under the guise of surrender).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: Where did mental fever arise, and which preparation was missing — offering, anchor, or desirelessness?
- **Weekly**: Is my baseline calm-while-acting rising? Which recurring desire or ego-claim keeps generating fever?
- **Monthly**: Are more of my actions becoming offerings (sannyasa) by default, with the mind naturally resting in the Self?

### Core rule guardrail
- The verse says *yudhyasva* — "fight." If your practice quietly turns into withdrawal, passivity, or excuse-making, you have kept the renunciation in the wrong place. Renounce the **fever, the desire, and the ego** — never the **duty**. Restore full engagement first; then keep the mind cool.

---

## Operating Sequence

1. **Surrender (sannyasa)**: offer every action to the Higher; you are not its anxious owner.
2. **Anchor (adhyatma-chetasa)**: rest the mind in the Self before and during the work.
3. **Release (nirashih, nirmama)**: drop expectation of fruit and the sense of "mine."
4. **Act (yudhyasva)**: do your duty fully and decisively — detachment lives in the mind, not in idleness.
5. **Scan (vigata-jwara)**: monitor for mental fever; trace any heat to desire or ego and dissolve it.
6. **Return**: at day's end, offer back the actions and review where the fever entered.
