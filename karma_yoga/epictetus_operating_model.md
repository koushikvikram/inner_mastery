# Epictetus Operating Model — The Bhagavad Gita, *Karma Yoga (2.47)*


![Epictetus Operating Model — The Bhagavad Gita, *Karma Yoga (2.47)*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: the doctrine is compressed into one verse — *"Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana"*: **you have a right to action alone, never to its fruits**. Do not let the fruit (*phala*) be your motive, and do not be attached to inaction either. The path is **action without attachment to results** (*nishkama karma*): perform your **own duty** (*svadharma*) wholeheartedly, surrender the harvest, and act from **equanimity** (*samatva*) — "yoga is skill in action." Work becomes worship when the doer releases ownership of outcomes; the same deed that binds when craved liberates when offered. The unit of control is the *action you choose now*; the fruit belongs to time, law, and the field of forces beyond you.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Act with full effort while holding **no claim on the fruit** — right to the deed, not the result.
- Do **svadharma** — your own proper work — rather than an imitation of another's.
- Stay **even** in success and failure, gain and loss, praise and blame (*samatvam yoga uchyate*).
- Convert ordinary work into **worship** by offering each action and releasing its outcome.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Engage the action, renounce the fruit**: bring 100% to the doing; bring 0% demand to the result.
- **Refuse the two errors**: not craving the fruit, *and* not fleeing into inaction — "let not your attachment be to inaction."
- **Locate motive in duty, not reward**: ask "is this mine to do?" not "what will I get?"
- **Offer before acting**: dedicate the work, so the ego is not the owner of the harvest.
- **Hold equanimity as the discipline**, not the leftover feeling — practice evenness on purpose.

### Routines / checklists

- **Dedication at the start of work (1 min)**
  - Name the action and the role it serves (*svadharma*).
  - Say: "I have a right to this effort, not to its fruit."
  - Offer the work — release the result to what governs results.

- **Fruit-detachment check (before any task with a stake)**
  - Write the outcome you're craving (the *phala*).
  - Set it down: "this is not mine to grasp; the doing is."
  - Re-enter the task aimed at *quality of action*, not at the prize.

- **Equanimity drill (when results land)**
  - Win or loss arrived — name it plainly.
  - Apply *samatva*: "the same to me in success and failure."
  - Do not let the result rewrite the next action's motive.

- **Svadharma alignment (weekly)**
  - List this week's main labors.
  - Mark each: my proper duty, or a borrowed one chased for its fruit?
  - "Better one's own duty done imperfectly than another's done well."

- **Work-as-worship pass (daily close)**
  - Recall the day's main deeds as offerings, not transactions.
  - Note any place ego claimed ownership of an outcome — return it.

### What good execution looks like
- Effort is whole-hearted while the grip on results is visibly loose.
- You can name the *duty* behind a task without reaching first for the reward.
- Wins don't inflate and losses don't deflate the next action's commitment.
- Inaction is never used as an escape disguised as detachment.
- The day's work reads as offerings, not as bargains for fruit.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Right to action, not to fruit**: results depend on many causes; claiming them invites bondage and grief, while owning the deed keeps you free and effective.
- **Attachment binds, offering liberates**: it is not work but *craving for the fruit* that chains the doer.
- **Equanimity is yoga**: *samatvam yoga uchyate* — evenness of mind in dualities is the skillful state from which good action flows.
- **Svadharma over imitation**: your own duty, fitted to you, is safer and truer than another's path pursued for reward.
- **Skill in action**: *yogah karmasu kaushalam* — yoga shows up as the quality and steadiness of the doing.
- **The fruit motive corrupts the deed**: aim at the harvest and the work degrades into anxiety; aim at the work and the harvest takes care of itself.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you're calculating the reward before starting → drop the calculation; ask only "is this my duty, done well?"
- If a result disturbs you (elation or collapse) → you were attached to the fruit; reapply equanimity.
- If you're tempted to abandon the work because the outcome is uncertain → that is attachment to inaction; act anyway.
- If you're copying another's role for its payoff → return to svadharma.
- If ego claims credit/blame for an outcome → re-offer the action and release ownership.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, action-tied)
- **Dedicated actions / day** (tasks begun with offering + fruit-release).
- **Fruit-craving catches / week** (times you noticed and set down a result-craving before acting).
- **Equanimity holds** (results that did *not* swing your next action's motive).
- **Svadharma alignment %** (share of effort on proper duty vs. borrowed, reward-chased work).
- **Inaction-escape incidents** (target → 0: refusals to act dressed up as "detachment").

### Review loop
- **Daily**: Which actions did I offer, and which did I secretly do for the fruit? Where did a result throw me off even?
- **Weekly**: Is my effort on *svadharma*, or am I chasing another's harvest? Where was attachment strongest?
- **Monthly**: Has my baseline evenness in gain/loss improved? Is more of my work becoming worship rather than transaction?

### Core rule guardrail
- If detachment becomes an excuse for half-effort or for not acting at all, the verse has been inverted. The renunciation is of the *fruit*, never of the *work*. Restore full effort first, then release the result — never the reverse.

---

## Operating Sequence

1. **Dedicate**: name the duty, offer the action, declare your right to effort and not to fruit.
2. **Act fully**: bring complete effort and *kaushalam* (skill) to the doing.
3. **Detach the fruit**: set down the craved result; refuse both the reward-motive and the escape into inaction.
4. **Meet results evenly**: apply *samatva* to gain and loss alike.
5. **Realign to svadharma**: confirm the work is your proper duty, not a borrowed one chased for its prize.
6. **Review as worship**: at day's end, return any outcome the ego claimed, and renew the offering.
