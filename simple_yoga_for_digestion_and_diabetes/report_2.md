## Answer Key

1.  **Question:** What is the recommended duration for sitting in a specific posture after eating?
    **Answer:** It is recommended to sit in the specific posture for 10 minutes following a meal. This duration is presented as a sufficient amount of time to ensure proper health benefits.

2.  **Question:** What are the primary health benefits associated with sitting in the recommended posture after a meal?
    **Answer:** Sitting in this manner ensures that food is properly digested and prevents the individual from falling ill. It is described as a fundamental practice for maintaining overall physical well-being.

3.  **Question:** Which specific asana is recommended for individuals suffering from "sugar" (diabetes)?
    **Answer:** Mandukasana is specifically highlighted as the exercise for those dealing with sugar or diabetes. It is performed in conjunction with Vajrasana to address these specific health concerns.

4.  **Question:** How is the thumb positioned during the performance of Mandukasana?
    **Answer:** During Mandukasana, the thumb is placed in a specific, designated spot as part of the posture. This placement is a key component of the technique designed for those with diabetes.

5.  **Question:** What are the three essential asanas identified as being life-saving?
    **Answer:** The three essential asanas are Malasana, Ukdu Asana, and Vajrasana. These three specific postures are described as being powerful enough to save a person's life.

6.  **Question:** According to the source, what is the long-term benefit of performing the three recommended asanas regularly?
    **Answer:** Performing these three asanas daily eliminates the need for frequent visits to doctors. They are presented as a complete system for maintaining health without external medical reliance.

7.  **Question:** What is the single pranayama recommended to complement the physical asanas?
    **Answer:** The only breathing exercise recommended is Anulom-Vilom. If one chooses to perform any pranayama at all, this is the specific one identified as being necessary.

8.  **Question:** How is the breathing technique for Anulom-Vilom described?
    **Answer:** The technique involves inhaling through one side (nostril) and exhaling through the other side. This simple alternating breath pattern constitutes the entirety of the recommended pranayama practice.

9.  **Question:** What is the significance of the "four" specific practices mentioned at the end of the text?
    **Answer:** The four practices—consisting of three asanas and one pranayama—are collectively described as life-saving. Engaging in these four daily is presented as a comprehensive routine for a healthy life.

10. **Question:** How does the source characterize the necessity of medical intervention for those who follow this yoga routine?
    **Answer:** The source suggests that those who practice these asanas will not have to go through the cycle of visiting doctors. The routine is framed as a preventative measure that secures health independently.

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Anulom-Vilom** | A specific pranayama (breathing exercise) characterized by inhaling through one nostril and exhaling through the other. |
| **Asana** | A physical posture or position used in yoga to improve health and wellness. |
| **Malasana** | One of the three primary asanas recommended for life-saving health benefits; also known as the Garland Pose. |
| **Mandukasana** | Also known as the Frog Pose, this asana involves specific thumb placement and is specifically recommended for managing diabetes. |
| **Pranayama** | The practice of breath control; the text identifies Anulom-Vilom as the essential form of this practice. |
| **Ukdu Asana** | A squatting posture identified as one of the three essential exercises for maintaining health and avoiding medical visits. |
| **Vajrasana** | The Thunderbolt Pose; a sitting posture recommended for 10 minutes after meals to aid digestion and promote overall health. |
