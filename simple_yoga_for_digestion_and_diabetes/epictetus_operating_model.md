# Epictetus Operating Model — Simple Yoga for Digestion and Diabetes (3 Asanas + 1 Breath)

> Source frame: wellness has been over-sold as complexity (pills, supplements, hundreds of poses and pranayamas), but metabolic resilience is reclaimed by a **10-minute return to foundational movement**. The minimalist prescription is **three asanas and one breath**: **Vajrasana** (Thunderbolt — sit on the heels 10 minutes *after eating* to re-route energy to digestion: "sit like this; you will never get sick and your food will be digested"); **Mandukasana** (Frog — from Vajrasana, thumbs tucked in fists pressed into the navel, fold forward to put precise pressure on the pancreas — the "Metabolic Shield"); **Malasana / Ukdu Asan** (the deep squat — foundational for elimination and grounding, lost in a chair-bound society); and **Anulom-Vilom** (alternate-nostril breathing — ignore the sea of pranayamas, master this one to balance the nervous system). "These four alone can save your life." The unit of control is a disciplined **10-minute commitment** to sit, squat, and breathe — beating decision fatigue by stripping away the non-essential.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Reclaim metabolic and digestive health through **foundational movement**, not complexity.
- Re-route energy to digestion and put **targeted pressure on the pancreas**.
- Restore the lost **deep squat** for elimination and grounding.
- Balance the nervous system with a single, repeatable breath.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Sit in Vajrasana after eating**: 10 minutes on the heels, immediately post-meal.
- **Press the pancreas in Mandukasana**: thumbs in fists into the navel, fold forward for internal pressure.
- **Reclaim the deep squat**: descend low in Malasana/Ukdu Asan, not a shallow gym squat.
- **Master one breath**: practice Anulom-Vilom daily; ignore the noise of hundreds of techniques.
- **Keep it to ~10 minutes**: strip the non-essential; beat decision fatigue with minimalism.
- **Practice consistently**: the trio is a daily barrier against chronic illness, not a one-off.

### Routines / checklists

- **The post-meal sequence (~10 minutes)**
  - Vajrasana — sit on the heels for 10 minutes after eating to drive energy into digestion.
  - Mandukasana — stay in Vajrasana, tuck thumbs inside fists, press into the navel, fold the torso forward over the hands (pancreas/abdominal compression).
  - Malasana (Ukdu Asan) — deep low squat, compress the core, open the hips (intake → processing → elimination complete).

- **The single breath (daily)**
  - Anulom-Vilom — inhale through one nostril, exhale through the other; rhythmic exchange to balance the nervous system and energy channels.

- **Minimalist discipline check**
  - Am I adding non-essential poses/supplements, or holding the four essentials?
  - Is the routine staying inside the ~10-minute commitment?

### What good execution looks like
- Vajrasana follows meals as a default, not an afterthought.
- Mandukasana delivers felt pressure on the abdomen/pancreas, not a vague stretch.
- The squat is genuinely deep, restoring elimination and grounding.
- One breath is practiced consistently instead of dabbling in many.
- The whole practice stays simple, repeatable, and ~10 minutes.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **The body has inherent intelligence**: modern life taught us to ignore foundational movement.
- **Stillness aids digestion**: Vajrasana re-routes energy so food is broken down, not left heavy and toxic.
- **Mechanical pressure regulates**: Mandukasana's navel compression targets the pancreas directly.
- **The squat is foundational**: intake, processing, and elimination depend on reclaiming Ukdu Asan.
- **Minimalism defeats decision fatigue**: hundreds of options stop people from starting; one breath everyone can do.
- **Discipline, not funds or knowledge, is the barrier**: the only cost is a 10-minute commitment.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you just ate, sit in Vajrasana — don't walk it off or lie down.
- If managing blood sugar, add Mandukasana from the Vajrasana seat for pancreatic pressure.
- If you've lost mobility, reclaim the deep squat instead of a shallow substitute.
- If overwhelmed by pranayama choices, do Anulom-Vilom and ignore the rest.
- If tempted to add complexity, strip back to the four essentials.
- If you "don't have time," remember it's the length of one social-media scroll.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-first)
- **Post-meal Vajrasana rate**: meals followed by 10 minutes on the heels.
- **Mandukasana consistency**: sessions with genuine navel/pancreatic compression.
- **Squat depth/restoration**: progress reclaiming a full Ukdu Asan.
- **Breath consistency**: days practicing Anulom-Vilom.
- **Simplicity adherence**: staying within the four essentials and ~10 minutes.
- **Outcome signals**: steadier digestion and blood-sugar readings over time.

### Review loop
- **Per meal**: did Vajrasana (and Mandukasana when needed) follow eating?
- **Daily**: did the single breath happen? Did the squat get reclaimed a little more?
- **Weekly**: is the routine staying minimalist, or has complexity crept back in?
- **Monthly**: are digestion and metabolic markers trending better on this simple practice?

### Core rule guardrail
- If you find yourself reaching for more poses, more supplements, or more pranayamas, you've drifted from the doctrine — return to sit, squat, and breathe, because the barrier is never knowledge or funds, only the discipline to keep it to a 10-minute commitment.
