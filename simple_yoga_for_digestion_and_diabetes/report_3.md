## The Triple-Asana Protocol

The source identifies three specific yoga postures as the foundation of physical health. These postures are described as essential for maintaining the body's digestive and metabolic functions.

### 1. Vajrasana (Thunderbolt Pose)
*   **Application:** This posture should be practiced for 10 minutes immediately after consuming a meal.
*   **Primary Benefit:** It is presented as a definitive solution for food digestion. 
*   **Long-term Outcome:** Consistent practice is claimed to prevent illness and ensure that food is processed efficiently by the body.

### 2. Mandukasana (Frog Pose)
*   **Target Demographic:** Specifically recommended for individuals suffering from diabetes ("Sugar").
*   **Methodology:** The practice involves placing the thumbs in a specific position (at the navel) while seated in Vajrasana.
*   **Strategic Importance:** It is categorized as the primary corrective posture for metabolic regulation in diabetic patients.

### 3. Malasana (Garland Pose / Ukdu Asan)
*   **Classification:** Identified as the third pillar of the physical protocol.
*   **Significance:** Along with Vajrasana and Mandukasana, Malasana is described as a "life-saving" posture that forms a complete physical health system.

## Respiratory Regulation: Anulom-Vilom Pranayama

For practitioners seeking to incorporate respiratory exercises into their routine, the source recommends a single, specific technique: **Anulom-Vilom**.

*   **Technique:** The practice involves a rhythmic breathing pattern where the practitioner inhales through one nostril and exhales through the opposite side.
*   **Sufficiency:** The source emphasizes that this single *pranayama* is sufficient; no other breathing exercises are required if this is performed daily.
