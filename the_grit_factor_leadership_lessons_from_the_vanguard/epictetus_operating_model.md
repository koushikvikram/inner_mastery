# Epictetus Operating Model — Shannon Huffman Polson, *The Grit Factor*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Shannon Huffman Polson, *The Grit Factor*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: grit is not innate — it is a layered crucible: **COMMIT → LEARN → LAUNCH**. **Own your narrative arc** (Kennedy: "If you don’t like someone’s story, you write your own."). Drill down to **bedrock purpose** with the **Five Whys** — connect head (task) to heart (impact). Build a **Solar System** of connections: mentors, dream team (anchor + reverse mentor), role models — and **draw the circle** to decide who stays out. **Listen Like a Leader**: **Ask → Listen (ting: ears, eyes, mind, heart) → Pause** (3-Second / 24-Second / 24-Hour rule). Practice **Muddy-Boots Leadership** (MBWA, focused on people). **Point your nose into the wind** — confront the brutal facts (Stockdale Paradox) while keeping faith in the prevail. Out-train the resistance.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Author your story instead of inheriting one.
- Operate from a **bedrock purpose** clear enough to survive the dark moments.
- Curate a deliberate **Solar System** — mentors, anchors, reverse mentors, role models.
- Listen with discipline (Ask → Listen → Pause) before deciding.
- Face into the wind — confront brutal facts while holding the prevail.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Re-write your narrative arc** when the script handed to you doesn’t fit.
- **Run the Five Whys** at every major commitment.
- **Map your Solar System** — and prune it.
- **Listen with *ting***: ears, eyes, mind, heart.
- **Turn your nose into the wind** when easier moves exist.

### Routines / checklists

- **Narrative arc rewrite (quarterly, 30 min)**
  - Read your current "story" — the one others (or you) are telling.
  - Identify the script you didn’t choose.
  - Write the next chapter in your own words: protagonist, struggle, action, outcome.

- **Five Whys on commitment (before any major effort)**
  - Why am I doing this? → Why does that matter? (× 5)
  - Stop when you reach a sentence that hits the chest, not just the head.
  - That’s the bedrock. Anchor the work to it.

- **Solar System mapping (quarterly)**
  - **Center (Mentors)**: shared purpose; can be different background, must share values.
  - **Inner orbit (Dream Team)**: Anchor (holistic supporter) + Reverse Mentor (gives honest feedback up).
  - **Far stars (Role Models)**: distant, fictional or historical, source of inspiration.
  - **Outside the circle**: who needs to stay out — for energy, focus, mission protection.
  - Update the map. Reach out to one person in each ring.

- **Listen Like a Leader (in every important conversation)**
  - **Ask**: open, vulnerable question — *"What do you think?" "What’s hard about this?"*
  - **Listen** (*ting*): ears (words), eyes (body), mind (undivided attention), heart (intent).
  - **Pause**:
    - **3-second rule** — wait before reacting.
    - **24-second / 24-hour rule** — for charged decisions, wait longer.

- **Muddy-Boots round (weekly)**
  - Get out to where the work is happening.
  - Don’t open with problems — open with people.
  - Listen. Take one note worth bringing back.

- **Nose-into-the-wind move (weekly)**
  - Identify one resistance you’re tempted to avoid.
  - Out-train it: prepare so your bad day exceeds the average good day.
  - Face the wind; let the lift carry you.

- **Stockdale check (in any sustained adversity)**
  - **Faith**: maintain absolute conviction you will prevail in the end.
  - **Brutal facts**: simultaneously confront the worst of the current reality — no denial, no false hope.
  - Combine both. Optimism without facts is fantasy; facts without faith is despair.

### What good execution looks like
- You can name your bedrock purpose in one chest-hitting sentence.
- Your Solar System is intentional — and pruned.
- In conversations, you Ask and Pause more than you Assert.
- You go to the work, not just hear about it.
- When resistance comes, you turn the nose into it on schedule, not by accident.
- You hold prevail and brutal facts simultaneously.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Narrative identity is choice**: the story you tell shapes the actions you take.
- **Purpose is a survival tactic**: in the dark moments, only bedrock holds.
- **Networks are managed, not collected**: who you let close shapes your energy.
- **Listening is leverage**: most leaders react before understanding; the disciplined leader pauses.
- **Lift comes from facing the wind**: an Apache rises only by turning into resistance.
- **Stockdale Paradox**: hope without realism breaks; realism without hope kills.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If a story is being told *to* you, write the next chapter yourself.
- If you can’t answer "why" five times, the commitment isn’t bedrock.
- If your Solar System is unintentional, it’s probably draining you.
- If you’re responding fast, you’re probably responding shallow — pause.
- If you’re tempted to turn back, turn the nose into the wind instead.
- If you have only optimism (or only facts), you’re half-equipped — combine.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Narrative-rewrite events / year**.
- **Five-Whys runs / quarter** (per major commitment).
- **Solar System updates / quarter** (additions and removals).
- **Ask : Assert ratio** in important conversations.
- **Pause adherence** (3-sec / 24-sec / 24-hr rules used).
- **Nose-into-wind moves / month**.
- **Stockdale balance score** (self-rated 1–5, faith × facts).

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: where did I assert when I should have asked?
- **Monthly**: did I face one wind I could have ducked?
- **Quarterly**: refresh narrative + bedrock + Solar System.

### Core rule guardrail
- If your hand keeps reaching for the easier vector, the model has slackened. Choose one resistance this week and *out-train it* deliberately. Without facing wind, there is no lift, and grit decays into routine.
