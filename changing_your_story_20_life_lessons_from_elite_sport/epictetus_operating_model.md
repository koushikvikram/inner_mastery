# Epictetus Operating Model — Bill Beswick, *Changing Your Story*

> Source frame: humans are storytelling creatures, and the internal narrative *is* the architect of performance. **The mind is the athlete**; the body is the means. Real change starts when you **cross the line** — taking total responsibility (Belichick: do what you’re supposed to do, when, where). The operating distinction is **Fighter vs. Victim** — fighters are active, solution-focused; victims look for a place to hide. Mastery is **raising the bottom line** (closing the gap between 60% and 90%, not chasing 100%). Momentum is built via the **Tick Stepladder** of small, "look-the-part" actions. Stay in the **Green** by checking the **HATED** factors (Hungry / Angry / Tired / Emotional / Dehydrated). The five keys to a sustainable story: **Purpose, People, Place, Health, Finance**.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Replace the inherited story with one you author and execute.
- Train the *mind* with the same rigor as the body.
- Convert a Victim mentality into a Fighter mentality on demand.
- Raise your bottom line so even your worst day is high-quality.
- Sustain it via Purpose, People, Place, Health, Finance.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Cross the Line daily**: own your response, drop excuses.
- **Train the mind first**, body second.
- **Use the Tick Stepladder**: tiny, daily, identity-aligned actions.
- **Run the HATED check** before reacting in any high-stakes moment.
- **Audit the Five Keys** weekly so the story stays sustainable.

### Routines / checklists

- **Morning "Cross the Line" reset (3–5 min)**
  - State today’s line: *"I will do what I’m supposed to do, when and where I’m supposed to."*
  - Commit one Fighter action and one act of taking responsibility.
  - Identify and drop one excuse you were carrying from yesterday.

- **Tick Stepladder (daily, micro-actions)**
  - Choose 3 ticks for today, identity-aligned and tiny:
    - Arrive early to one thing.
    - Look the part for one thing (presentation, message, environment).
    - Greet/connect with one person who matters.
  - Mark the ticks at end of day.

- **Bottom-line raise drill (weekly)**
  - Identify what your "60%" looks like (worst-day output).
  - Identify what your "90%" looks like (best honest output).
  - Pick ONE habit that raises the 60% (e.g., a non-negotiable warm-up).
  - Track only the bottom line for 30 days; ignore peaks.

- **HATED check (before reactive moments)**
  - Hungry? Angry / alcohol-affected? Tired? Emotional? Dehydrated?
  - If yes to any → fix the physical first; postpone the reaction.
  - Then interpret the situation rationally, not reactively.

- **Traffic light state-check (3× per day)**
  - Green: focused, dealing.
  - Amber: warning — start managing.
  - Red: reacting — pause, breathe, run HATED.
  - Goal: spend the day in Green, with deliberate Amber recoveries.

- **Reframe drill (when in a "perfect storm")**
  - Take a mental time-out → rise to "helicopter view."
  - From height: what is the *opportunity* hidden in this?
  - Re-enter as Fighter, not Victim.

- **End-of-day storytelling check (5 min)**
  - In one sentence: what was today’s chapter?
  - Was I the hero, the passenger, or the victim of this chapter?
  - What is the first sentence of tomorrow’s chapter?

- **Five Keys audit (weekly, 10 min)**
  - **Purpose**: clear reason to get out of bed?
  - **People**: who supported / drained me?
  - **Place**: did the environment help?
  - **Health**: physical, mental, emotional baseline?
  - **Finance**: enough security to remove uncontrollable stress?

### What good execution looks like
- You catch and reframe Victim language quickly — same hour, not same week.
- The Tick Stepladder is filling up daily; momentum is visible.
- Your bottom-line work is clearly higher than three months ago.
- Reactive moments end with HATED-checked, Green-state responses.
- Your weekly story has a clear protagonist — you.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **The mind is the athlete**: physical preparation is entry fee; mental discipline is the differentiator.
- **Responsibility is the foundation of strength**: until you cross the line, no other tool works.
- **Mastery beats perfectionism**: 100% is a prison; raising the floor is freedom.
- **Momentum is built ladder-rung by ladder-rung**: small ticks compound into identity.
- **Emotions are choices, not reflexes**: the gap between trigger and response is trainable.
- **Bandwidth is biological**: HATED factors hijack interpretation before you’re aware.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you hear a Victim sentence in your head, reframe before you act.
- If you’re aiming for 100%, redirect to raising the 60% instead.
- If you’re reactive, run HATED before deciding anything.
- If "I should" appears, replace with "I will, by [time/place]."
- If the story is stuck, change the next sentence — not the whole script.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Cross-the-line reps / week** (explicit ownership acts).
- **Tick stepladder completion rate**.
- **Bottom-line score** (worst-day output, week over week).
- **Green-time %** (estimated portion of day in Green state).
- **HATED-check usage / week** (in reactive moments).
- **Five Keys audit completed / month**.

### Review loop
- **Daily**: end-of-day story sentence + tomorrow’s first sentence.
- **Weekly**: Five Keys audit + bottom-line check + Tick consistency.
- **Monthly**: rewrite the operating story (one paragraph) — updated for who you’ve become.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re telling people about Fighter mentality but operating as a Victim in your inner narrative, the model isn’t doing anything. Re-anchor by writing tomorrow’s first sentence yourself, by hand, tonight.
