# Epictetus Operating Model — Jim Afremow, *The Champion’s Mind*

> Source frame: mentality separates the top few from the many — train the brain with the same clinical rigor as the body. **Mirror Effect**: admiration is a *psychological scouting report* — you can only recognize traits you already carry. **Feed the Good Wolf** with two distinct fuels — **Instructional self-talk** (novel / fine motor) and **Motivational self-talk** (strength / endurance). **Pressure is a privilege**, not a problem — the racing heart is the **Pumped** response, not the Panic response. Be a **Now-ist** — past = regret fog, future = worry fog, present = clear skies; **Win the Day** with morning intentions and evening accountability. Adopt a **Mastery orientation** over an **Ego orientation** — chase peace of mind, not the trophy. Bronze–Silver–Gold goals; *Think it, Ink it*.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Train mentality with clinical discipline — not vague positivity.
- Use admiration as a scouting report on your own latent traits.
- Convert pressure from threat to fuel.
- Win the day with intentions and accountability.
- Default to Mastery orientation; treat trophies as side effects.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Run the morning intention** ("How will I be a champion today?").
- **Run the evening accountability** ("How was I a champion today?").
- **Use the right self-talk for the task type** (instructional vs. motivational).
- **Reframe pressure** as preparation, not panic.
- **Default to mastery, not ego** in any decision about the work.

### Routines / checklists

- **Morning Champion Question (3 min)**
  - Ask: *"How will I be a champion today?"*
  - Set 1 process intention + 1 controllable behavior.
  - Visualize the *clear skies* — present-moment execution, not future outcome.

- **Mirror-Effect drill (weekly)**
  - List 3 people whose performance you admire and 1 specific trait each.
  - Recognize: that trait exists in seed form in you.
  - Plan one rep that develops it.

- **Self-talk selection (per task type)**
  - **Novel / fine motor / precision**: *Instructional* — technical cues ("full shoulder turn," "soft hands").
  - **Strength / endurance / push-through**: *Motivational* — power phrases ("Bring it on now," "Yes, I can").
  - Don’t mix them — wrong fuel undermines execution.

- **Pressure reframe (in any high-stakes moment)**
  - Notice the surge — heart rate, sweat, butterflies.
  - State: *"My body is preparing itself to perform."*
  - Get the butterflies to fly in formation; don’t kill them.

- **Now-ist reset (when foggy)**
  - Identify the fog: *past regret* or *future worry*?
  - Discard. Anchor in the present action.
  - Take the next concrete step.

- **Bronze–Silver–Gold goal-setting (per session / project)**
  - **Bronze**: minimum acceptable result.
  - **Silver**: realistic strong result.
  - **Gold**: stretch result.
  - Always achieve Bronze; aim for Gold; never call Bronze a failure.

- **Mastery vs. Ego decision check (in choice points)**
  - Ego asks: *what makes me look good?*
  - Mastery asks: *what makes me better?*
  - Choose Mastery. The trophies tend to follow.

- **Evening Champion Question (3 min)**
  - Ask: *"How was I a champion today?"*
  - One process win, one slip, one adjustment for tomorrow.

### What good execution looks like
- Morning and evening champion questions are answered honestly, daily.
- You match self-talk type to task type without thinking.
- Pre-performance nerves arrive and you welcome them.
- You stay present; you can name the fog when it shows up and discard it.
- Your goals run Bronze–Silver–Gold; Bronze is always banked.
- Decisions favor mastery, not ego — and you can name examples.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Mentality, not muscle, separates the top few**: physical preparation is the entry fee.
- **Admiration is recognition**: you can’t see what you don’t already carry the seed of.
- **Self-talk works mechanically**: instructional and motivational fuels do different jobs; type-mismatch underperforms.
- **Pressure is a privilege**: the body’s alarm is the body’s readiness.
- **The present is the only place performance happens**: past and future are noise.
- **Mastery is durable; ego is brittle**: external validation creates emptiness even after wins.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you’re fighting the butterflies, you’re wasting energy — get them to fly in formation.
- If you’re under-performing on a fine-motor task, your motivational self-talk is the wrong fuel.
- If you’re hesitating, you’re in fog — return to the present action.
- If you only celebrate Gold, you’ll burn out — bank Bronze every time.
- If your goal asks "what will people think?" you’re Ego-oriented — switch to Mastery.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Champion Question pairs / week** (morning + evening).
- **Self-talk type-match rate** (correct fuel chosen for the task).
- **Pressure reframes / week** (panic → pumped conversions).
- **Now-ist resets / week** (fog detected and dropped).
- **Bronze hit rate** (process floor consistently met).
- **Mastery : Ego decision ratio** (deliberate choices favoring mastery).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: champion question pair.
- **Weekly**: which self-talk type carried me; which slipped?
- **Monthly**: did mastery-decisions outnumber ego-decisions?

### Core rule guardrail
- If your motivation is "look how I look winning" — Ego has hijacked Mastery. Pause, return to the process, run the next session for peace of mind. Ego-orientation produces emptiness no medal can fill.
