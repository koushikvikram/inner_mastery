# Epictetus Operating Model — Sourav Ganguly, *A Century Is Not Enough*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Sourav Ganguly, *A Century Is Not Enough*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: in elite arenas the path is binary — "**India or nothing.**" There is no lateral safety net. Survival in this single-option world is built on **cognitive pre-scripting** (visualizing the specific delivery, the specific shot), **zero-ego investment** (treating minor fixtures with Test-match intensity), **psychological safety as leadership currency** ("I shall back you even if you fail"), and **calculated counter-attack** (*Bapi bari ja* — step out toward the threat, fly higher than the storm). Resilience over revenge. Pride over survival. Darkness has an expiry date.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Operate as if the path is binary (India or nothing) — full commitment, no hedging.
- Pre-script high-pressure outcomes so execution doesn’t collapse under stress.
- Treat every "minor" arena with maximum intensity to condition the mind for any stage.
- Lead by absorbing risk for others so they can play no-holds-barred.
- Counter-attack instead of playing safe when the back is against the wall.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Visualize the win, not the fear** the night before any major performance.
- **Zero-ego floor**: never coast in low-stakes work; play it like a Test match.
- **Step out, don’t crouch**: when threatened, attack the line.
- **Back people in writing/words** before they have to ask.
- **Stay in the present, fight for pride** when in a slump.

### Routines / checklists

- **Pre-event Cognitive Pre-Scripting (10–15 min, night before)**
  - Visualize 3 specific scenarios, 3 specific successful responses (e.g., "Lillee delivery → cover drive at the Gabba" specificity).
  - Replace "edge to keeper" rehearsals with "ball middled, watch it run" rehearsals.
  - Sleep on the picture of the desired execution, not the dreaded one.

- **Zero-Ego Drill (weekly, in low-stakes settings)**
  - Pick one "JP Atrya"-tier task (a minor meeting, internal review, side project).
  - Bring Test-match preparation: same notes, same intensity, same review.
  - Goal: condition the mind for the worst-case so the top tier feels easy.

- **Bapi bari ja counter-attack protocol (when cornered / criticized / behind)**
  - Identify the safe, defensive response — and discard it.
  - Take the attacking action: ship the bold work, defend the team in public, take the contrarian call.
  - Operating principle: dark clouds → fly higher.

- **Psychological-safety leadership move (in every team interaction)**
  - For each person you depend on this week, deliver one explicit "I’ll back you even if this fails" message.
  - Public defense, private debrief.
  - Outcome: extracts no-holds-barred performance.

- **Bisharjon / End reset (after major loss or ending)**
  - Allow the immersion (grief) but set a hard exit.
  - Move to "what is the next chapter" within the same week.
  - Refuse the trap of revenge; choose resilience instead.

### What good execution looks like
- You can describe tomorrow’s key moment in pre-scripted detail before it happens.
- You don’t pick which arenas to take seriously — all of them get full intensity.
- People you lead can name the moment you took risk for them.
- After a setback, your action is *attack* the next opportunity, not retreat.

---

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Single-option pressure changes everything**: when there’s no Plan B, identity and effort align.
- **Mental rehearsal beats positive thinking**: specificity wins, slogans don’t.
- **Pressure is the engine of meaning** (the fisherman analogy): the high-stakes life is the fuller life.
- **Conditioning to the worst makes the top tier feel like ease**: the "Rolls-Royce" feels normal once you’ve "slept on the pavement" mentally.
- **Leaders unlock performance by absorbing risk**: backing under fire is the currency that buys loyalty.
- **Defiance scales**: stepping out toward the bowler is more often correct than ducking.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you’re hedging, you’re leaking energy — pick a single line and commit.
- If you’re fearing the edge to the keeper, switch the rehearsal to the cover drive.
- If you’re tempted to coast in a "minor" task, treat it as a Test match instead.
- If a teammate is wobbling, defend them publicly first, debrief them privately later.
- If you’re cornered, counter-attack — calculated, not reckless.

---

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Pre-script reps / week** (specific, scenario-level visualizations done before a stake).
- **Zero-ego sessions / week** (low-stakes work treated at full intensity).
- **Backing acts / week** (explicit "I back you" interventions for people you lead).
- **Counter-attack ratio** (when cornered: % of times you stepped out vs. ducked).
- **Recovery latency from a "bisharjon" moment** (days to first attacking move forward).

### Review loop
- **Weekly (10–15 min)**:
  - Was the pre-script specific or generic this week?
  - Where did I coast on a "minor" stage?
  - Whom did I back loudly?
  - Where did I retreat when I should have stepped out?
- **Quarterly**: pick one "darkness" you’re still holding — set its expiry date.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you are *talking about* commitment but secretly preserving a Plan B, delete the Plan B and run the next session as "India or nothing." Without that constraint, the rest of this model softens into self-help.
