# Step 4: Review Your Results

After your project is finished (or if you end up putting it on pause for some reason), you should spend a little time analyzing it.

- What went right?
- What went wrong?
- What should you do next time to avoid making those same mistakes?

Not all of your projects will be successful. Although the tendency is to blame willpower and motivation, **very often the problems with projects can be traced back to their conception.**

*Mastering the principles is a lifelong process.*

Even your successful projects are worth analyzing.

- They can often tell you more than your failures because the reasons a successful project succeeded are the very elements you want to retain and replicate for the future.

With ultralearning the goal isn’t merely to learn one skill or subject but to hone and enhance your overall learning process.

- Each successful project can be refined and improved for the next one.
