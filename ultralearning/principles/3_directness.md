Principle 3

# Directness: Go Straight Ahead

> Directness is the idea of learning being tied closely to the situation or context you want to use it in.
> 

Hallmark of most ultralearning projects.

The easiest way to learn directly is to simply spend a lot of time doing the thing you want to become good at.

Won't work for all projects.

- The "real" situation may be
    - infrequent
    - difficult
    - impossible to create
- In such situations, gradually increase directness to improve your performance.

## Transfer: Education's Dirty Secret

*Transfer happens when you learn something in one context and are able to use it in another context.*

We expect this of almost all learning efforts.

Harder when our knowledge is more limited.

- As we develop more knowledge and skill in an area, they become
    - more flexible
    - easier to apply outside the narrow contexts in which they were learned

## 2 ways directness overcomes the problem of transfer

1. Significantly reduces the need for far transfer.
2. May help with transfer to new situations
    - Many real-world situations share many subtle details with other real-life situations.

Learning something new depends on the myriad of tiny details of how that knowledge interacts with reality.

Build knowledge outward from the kernel of a real situation.

## How ultralearners avoid the problem of transfer and learn directly

- Learn by doing
    - If that's not possible, create an artificial project/environment to test your skills.
- What matters most here is that there is **substantially similarity** between
    - **cognitive features of the skill you're trying to master**
    - **the way you practice it**

Directness matters even in cases where getting a theoretical understanding is the goal.

Whereas the structures of our knowledge start out brittle, welded to the environments and contexts we learn them in, with more work and time, they can become flexible and can be applied more broadly.

Many ultralearners who have specialized in a smaller subset of fields are masters at transfer, no doubt this is largely due to their depth of knowledge which makes transfer easier to accomplish.

## 4 tactics to learn directly

1. Project-based Learning
2. Immersive Learning
3. The Flight Simulator Method
4. The Overkill Approach

## Learn Straight from the Source

Whenever you learn anything new, ask yourself where and how the knolwedge will manifest itself. If you can answer that, you can ask whether you're doing anything to tie what you're learning to that question.

## Appendix

**Project-based Learning**

- If you organize your learning around producing something, you're guaranteed to at least learn how to produce that thing.
- Many skills naturally lend themselves to projects that produce something at the end.
    - Examples
        - Engineering
        - Art
        - Design
        - Musical composition
        - Carpentry
        - Writing
- An intellectual topic can also be the basis of a project.
    - Eg. If you want to learn Military History, you can do a project to produce a thesis paper.

**Immersive Learning**

- **Immersion**
    - Surrounding yourself with the target environment in which the skill is practiced.
    - Advantage
        - Requires much larger amounts of practice than would be typical
        - Exposes you to a fuller range of situations in which the skill applies
    - Can also be done by joining communities of people who are actively engaged in learning.
        - Eg. Join open-source projects to expose yourself to coding challenges.

**The Flight Simulator Method**

- For many skills, immersion isn't possible.
    - Eg. Piloting a plane, performing surgery.
- In such cases, a simulation of the environment will work to the degree to which it remains faithful to the cognitive elements of the task in question.

**The Overkill Approach**

- Increase the challenge so that the skill level required is wholly contained within the goal that is set.
- Put yourself into an environment where the demands are going to be extremely high, so you're unlikely to miss any important lessons or feedback.
- Going into this environment can feel intense.
    - You many feel "not ready", "afraid" or want to procrastinate.
    - These fears are temporary.
    - If you can get enough motivation to start this method, it's often a lot easier to continue it long-term.
- One way to overkill a project is to aim for a particular test, performance or challenge that will be above the skill level you strictly require.
