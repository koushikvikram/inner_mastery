## Part 2: Answer Key

1.  **Istora Fever** refers to the intense, loud, and often hostile atmosphere created by Indonesian fans at the Istora Senayan stadium, which can make it impossible for players to hear their coaches or the score. Sen notes that while the crowd is "the worst" for opponents, it provides a great atmosphere that forces a player to learn how to silence the audience through performance.
2.  In his youth, Sen and his doubles partner wrote a **handwritten letter** to their coach, Vimal Sir, after losing multiple times to the same pair, pledging to work harder to beat them. This acted as a manifestation tool, teaching Sen at an early age to write down specific goals and plans for the next six months to a year.
3.  Sen aims to hit a **heart rate of 140–150 BPM** during his warmup to ensure his body is "gasping" and fully activated before the match begins. He believes that overthinking and mental fear occur more frequently when the heart rate is not at its optimal "flow" level, so reaching this state early allows for a free-flowing start.
4.  **3U rackets** are heavier (approximately 85 grams) and provide more power and "punch" for smashes, whereas **4U rackets** are lighter and preferred for modern speed and quick reflections. While 4U is more commonly available in India, Sen personally prefers 3U for the added control and power it offers in singles play.
5.  **Drift** is caused by high-powered air conditioning units in humid Asian stadiums, which creates wind that moves the shuttlecock unpredictably. Players must strategize based on whether they are playing "with the wind" (requiring more control) or "against the wind" (allowing for harder hits without the shuttle going out of bounds).
6.  **Inat** is described as a Serbian mindset involving an inner anger and a desire to prove doubters wrong. While some athletes operate from this place of defiance, Sen identifies more with a "calm and composed" mindset, though he acknowledges the satisfaction of silencing a crowd that expects him to lose.
7.  Under the guidance of his mental trainer, Sen **visualized himself losing** close sets to Shi Yuqi for several days to remove the fear of defeat from his mind. On the day of the match, he shifted to visualizing a win, which allowed him to play the actual game without the paralyzing pressure of his previous losing streak against that opponent.
8.  Following the Olympics and subsequent injuries, Sen faced a period of "desperation" where he lost in the first rounds of several tournaments. He eventually recovered by **withdrawing from competition** for two months to focus on physical healing and resetting his mindset to accept potential losses while fighting for every point.
9.  Sen uses a **breathwork technique** involving five seconds of inhaling and five seconds of exhaling during the short breaks between rallies. This helps him stabilize his heart rate, block out external distractions like umpire decisions or crowd noise, and refocus entirely on the next point.
10. Sen views his father, D.K. Sen, as someone who masterfully balances being a **coach on the court and a father at home**. He considers his father a vital part of his technical team but feels no extra pressure when he is watching, as his father prioritizes discipline and effort over the final result of the match.

## Part 4: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **3U / 4U** | Classification of badminton racket weights; 3U is heavier (approx. 85g) for power, while 4U is lighter for speed. |
| **All England** | One of the most prestigious badminton tournaments in the world; Sen reached the finals in 2022. |
| **Breathwork** | A tactical tool used by Sen (5 seconds in, 5 seconds out) to maintain a calm heart rate and mental focus between rallies. |
| **Drift** | The movement of the shuttlecock caused by indoor air currents, usually from air conditioning blowers in large stadiums. |
| **G5 / G4 / G3** | Measurements of racket grip thickness; Sen prefers a thicker grip (lower numbers) due to his larger hand size. |
| **Inat** | A Serbian term for a specific type of competitive anger used to prove critics wrong; a "proving them wrong" mindset. |
| **Istora Fever** | The intense, loud atmosphere at the Istora Senayan stadium in Indonesia, known for being challenging for non-local players. |
| **Manifestation Letter** | A practice Sen started at a young age, writing down goals and work plans to commit to future victories. |
| **SNC (Strength and Conditioning)** | Specialized physical training; Sen uses light SNC work on match days to ensure muscle activation without fatigue. |
| **Thomas Cup** | A major international team competition; India won the title in 2022, a milestone Sen cites as a major confidence booster. |
| **Visualization** | The mental rehearsal of match scenarios; Sen uses this to confront the fear of losing and to simulate winning close points. |
| **Winning Line Fever** | The psychological tension that occurs when a player is close to winning (e.g., at 19 or 20 points), often leading to stiff movement and errors. |
