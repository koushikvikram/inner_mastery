# Epictetus Operating Model — Paddy Upton, *The Barefoot Coach*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Paddy Upton, *The Barefoot Coach*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: dominance comes from the **radical surrender of the ego**. Become a "**complete nobody**" before leading; without that, expertise is a mask. **Stop instructing, start asking** — the **Power Paradox**: the more power a leader appropriates, the less actual influence they hold (90% of instructions forgotten in 3 months; 85% retention from coaching). Build excellence on **strengths, not fixed weaknesses** — the Sehwag move (teach ducking, don’t fix the hook). Find asymmetric advantage in the "**rats and mice**" (the tailender 83-run protocol). Innovation is **subtraction** — shed the "**blazers and ties**" of useless tradition; protect the player’s **storytelling** (Gambhir’s shoes). **Vulnerability** is a leadership superpower — dismantle the **myth of mental toughness** ("inner substance" over "outer show"). The crack is where the light gets in.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Lead from substance, not status — be a "complete nobody" inside.
- Replace instruction with **questions** that build thinking players.
- Multiply through **strengths**; manage rather than fix weaknesses.
- Find the asymmetric **rats-and-mice** advantage in your system.
- Innovate by **subtraction** — and protect the player’s storytelling.
- Treat vulnerability as a leadership tool, not a liability.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Drop the "looking good" mask**.
- **Ask first, instruct last.**
- **Build on strengths**; only neutralize critical weaknesses.
- **Audit "blazers and ties"** quarterly.
- **Protect storytelling rituals** — they often hold the neuromuscular execution.
- **Show fallibility** — give others permission to be authentic.

### Routines / checklists

- **Nobody check (weekly, 10 min)**
  - Where did I lead from status this week?
  - Strip the title: would my behavior still hold?
  - One act this week of operating without the credentials.

- **Coach not instructor (every 1:1)**
  - Replace **"Do X"** with **"What do you think we should do here?"**
  - Use the **85% rule**: aim for retention, not compliance.
  - Wait through silence; let them find their own answer.

- **Strengths-first audit (monthly)**
  - Per person you lead: list 3 strengths, 1 critical weakness.
  - Plan: 80% of development time → amplify strengths; 20% → neutralize the critical weakness.
  - Resist the corporate urge to "round them out" — round-them-out = average them out.

- **Sehwag move (in any improvement plan)**
  - Don’t fix the player’s natural high-risk move — protect the upside.
  - Find the *learned skill* (ducking) that neutralizes the *threat* without disabling the strength.
  - Outcome: the strength compounds; the weakness is contained.

- **Rats-and-mice scan (quarterly)**
  - Where in your system are the "tailenders" — under-invested-in but high-leverage?
  - Add 1 specific upgrade.
  - Track the upgrade as an asymmetric advantage (psychological warfare on the opposition).

- **Blazers and ties subtraction (quarterly)**
  - List rituals / processes / artifacts you maintain "because it’s always been done."
  - For each: would the best in 5 years still be doing this?
  - Subtract the answer that’s "no" — and watch what *doesn’t* happen.

- **Storytelling protection (in technical coaching)**
  - When a player has an irrational ritual that works (Gambhir’s shoes) — protect it.
  - Coach around it, not against it.
  - Your job is execution, not theoretical purity.

- **Vulnerability as practice (weekly)**
  - In one conversation, admit one thing you don’t know or got wrong.
  - Give others the permission to do the same.
  - Watch trust compound.

### What good execution looks like
- You can lead the room without invoking your title.
- People you coach are *thinking*, not waiting to be told.
- Improvement plans amplify strengths and contain critical weaknesses — they don’t average people out.
- One asymmetric "rats-and-mice" upgrade is in flight right now.
- A blazer-and-tie tradition was retired this quarter without anything breaking.
- People around you are admitting fallibility — because you did first.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **The ego is the bottleneck**: status protects looking-good; substance protects performance.
- **The Power Paradox**: appropriated power costs you actual influence.
- **Strengths multiply, weakness-fixes only catch you up**: excellence lives where the brain works easily.
- **Asymmetric advantage hides in the under-invested**: the tailenders carry the next gain.
- **Innovation is often subtraction**: useless tradition is the cheap source of capacity.
- **Stories drive execution**: rituals you can’t justify often produce results you can.
- **Vulnerability is structural**: it dismantles the toughness myth and unlocks inner substance.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you’re instructing, you’re probably leaking retention — switch to a question.
- If you’re fixing weakness, ask whether you should be amplifying strength instead.
- If everyone is doing the obvious, look at the rats-and-mice for asymmetric gain.
- If a tradition can’t justify itself, retire it.
- If a player has a working ritual, protect it — even if it’s "illogical."
- If you can’t admit a not-knowing in public, you’re wearing the mask.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Question : instruction ratio** (in 1:1s and team forums).
- **Strengths-amplification time** (% of development time).
- **Tailender upgrades / quarter** (rats-and-mice investments shipped).
- **Blazers retired / quarter**.
- **Vulnerability moments / week** (admissions made, permissions granted).
- **Storytelling rituals protected / quarter** (vs. "corrected").

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: where did I lead from title vs. substance?
- **Monthly**: who got coached vs. instructed — and what did they retain?
- **Quarterly**: subtract one tradition; invest in one tailender; measure the asymmetry.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re *talking* about athlete-centered coaching but still issuing edicts to look in control, you’ve put the mask back on. Become a nobody first — substance, then questions, then influence — or the model collapses into another "expert" performance.
