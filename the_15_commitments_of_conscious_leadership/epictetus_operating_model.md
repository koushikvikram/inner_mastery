# Epictetus Operating Model — Dethmer, Chapman & Klemp, *The 15 Commitments of Conscious Leadership*


![Epictetus Operating Model — Dethmer, Chapman & Klemp, *The 15 Commitments of Conscious Leadership*](./epictetus_operating_model.png)

> Source frame: at any nanosecond a leader is either **above the line** (open, curious, committed to learning) or **below the line** (closed, defensive, committed to being right) — there is no middle. Being below is a normal, **amygdala**-driven reaction to imagined threats to the ego. Leaders move through four states — **To Me** (victim), **By Me** (creator), **Through Me** (channel), **As Me** (oneness) — and the gateway from victimhood to creation is **radical responsibility**: locating cause inside, not blaming outward. The master skill is **drift/shift**: everyone drifts below the line; conscious leaders shift back fast. Supporting practices include exiting the **victim-villain-hero drama triangle**, feeling all feelings as **"e-motion"** (the 90-second rule), speaking **unarguably** (thoughts/feelings/sensations), eliminating **gossip**, and treating **integrity as wholeness**. Your commitments are revealed by your results, not your words.

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Operate above the line as the default, and shift back quickly when you drift below.
- Migrate from "To Me" victimhood to "By Me" creatorship through radical responsibility.
- Remove organizational "sludge" (drama, gossip, withholding) that drains energy.
- Use emotions as data, not noise — and keep the energy of integrity flowing.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Locate the line first**: before reacting, ask "Am I above or below the line right now?"
- **Take radical responsibility**: locate cause and control inside yourself, not in markets, boards, or people.
- **Exit the drama triangle**: refuse the Victim, Villain, and (most seductively) Hero roles.
- **Feel it, then release it**: honor the 90-second rule instead of recycling the emotion into a mood.
- **Speak unarguably**: report only thoughts, feelings, and sensations — not stories dressed as fact.
- **End gossip on contact**: say nothing about someone you wouldn't say to their face; as listener, end the game.
- **Read commitment by results**: assume you're committed to whatever your current reality is producing.

### Routines / checklists

- **Above/Below the Line check-in (anytime, especially under threat)**
  - Notice the body: tight, defensive, needing to be right = below.
  - Admit it out loud — self-awareness is the first mark of a conscious leader.
  - There is no gray; anything less than 100% open is below.

- **Shift moves (when you've drifted below)**
  - **Physical**: conscious breathing (4-second inhale / 4-second exhale); radical posture change to alter blood chemistry.
  - **Cognitive**: ask a **Wonder Question** — "I wonder what I can learn here?" — which has no right answer.
  - Re-enter curiosity, not conclusion.

- **Radical responsibility pass (when blaming)**
  - Catch the blame ("moving cause away").
  - Ask "How am I creating, promoting, or allowing this?"
  - Name your part; reclaim the locus of control.

- **90-second emotional release (e-motion)**
  - **Locate** the physical sensation in the body.
  - **Breathe** into it.
  - **Match**: vocalize or move to express the energy until it clears.
  - Read the wisdom: anger = boundary/sword; fear = pay attention; sadness = let go; joy = celebrate; sexual feelings = creativity.

- **Unarguable communication template (candor)**
  - "I'm having the thought that..."
  - "I feel [anger / fear / sadness / joy]..."
  - "I have a sensation of [physical description]..."
  - Separate **fact** (what a video camera records) from **story** (interpretation) and hold stories lightly.

- **Clearing model (instead of gossip)**
  - Take the issue directly to the person.
  - State facts, then your story, then your feelings and your want.
  - Check the payback you were chasing (validation, diverting attention, avoiding conflict, control).

### What good execution looks like
- Leaders openly name "I'm below the line" without shame and shift quickly.
- Blame language ("they/it/the market") is replaced with "my part is...".
- Emotions move through in seconds instead of hardening into bitterness loops.
- Conversations are unarguable; drama and gossip drop toward zero.
- The team "sees reality best" because withholding has stopped.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Above/below the line is binary**: in the psychology of performance, "mostly open" is still closed.
- **Below the line is the amygdala protecting the ego**, not reality — it shuts down collaboration and problem-solving.
- **Radical responsibility is the only gateway** from To Me to By Me; without it you stay at the effect of life.
- **The Hero is the most dangerous role**: over-functioning relieves tension short-term but keeps others in victimhood.
- **Emotions are energy in motion**: repressed or recycled, they drain cognitive bandwidth.
- **Withholding causes withdrawal and projection**: "you spot it, you got it."
- **Commitments are shown by results, not statements**: current reality is the honest scoreboard.
- **Integrity is wholeness**: congruence between inside experience and outside expression keeps energy flowing.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If you need to be right, you're below the line — shift before you decide.
- If you're blaming, find your part before doing anything else.
- If you're "helping" by over-functioning, you're playing Hero — stop and build capacity instead.
- If an emotion has lasted hours, you're recycling a story — locate, breathe, match.
- If you wouldn't say it to their face, it's gossip — don't say it at all.
- If you want to know your real commitment, look at your results, not your intentions.

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, presence-first)
- **Shift latency** (time from drifting below to shifting back above the line).
- **Responsibility ratio** (statements of ownership vs. blame in a given week).
- **Drama-triangle incidents** (Victim/Villain/Hero episodes logged and exited).
- **Emotional recycle time** (how long feelings linger past 90 seconds).
- **Gossip/withholding count** (instances caught and converted to direct clearing).
- **Integrity breaches** (unkept agreements, withheld truths — and how fast they're cleaned up).

### Review loop
- **Daily**: where was I below the line, and how fast did I shift?
- **Weekly**: drama-triangle and gossip review; one relationship to clear directly.
- **Monthly**: are my results revealing a commitment I won't admit out loud?
- **Quarterly**: integrity sweep — broken agreements renegotiated, incongruence closed.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you're tracking dashboards and rituals while still committed to being right, you're managing optics from below the line. Drop the need to be right, take radical responsibility, and shift back above the line — no metric matters until presence is restored.

---

## Operating Sequence

1. Locate the line — am I above or below right now?
2. If below, take radical responsibility and shift (breathe, posture, wonder question).
3. Feel and release any e-motion in 90 seconds; read its wisdom.
4. Speak unarguably, end gossip, and clear issues directly.
5. Read your results as your true commitments, then re-commit and repeat.
