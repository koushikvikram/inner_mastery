## Part 2: Quiz Answer Key

1.  **Definition of HIIT:** HIIT involves performing physical exercise in short, sharp bursts, typically pushing oneself for less than a minute. These bursts are followed by a short recovery break, and the cycle is repeated multiple times to achieve an effective workout in a short duration.
2.  **Cellular and Hormonal Benefits:** HIIT benefits the mitochondria, which are the power-packs providing energy to the body's cells. Additionally, it improves the body’s sensitivity to insulin, which helps reduce the risk of developing type 2 diabetes.
3.  **Visceral Fat Risks:** Visceral fat is a dangerous type of fat that coats internal organs and is largely invisible from the outside. It is associated with increased rates of heart attacks, strokes, and type 2 diabetes.
4.  **TOFI Explained:** TOFI stands for "Thin on the Outside, Fat on the Inside." It refers to individuals who appear thin but carry high, dangerous levels of internal visceral fat.
5.  **Workout Timing:** While the workouts can be done anytime, the source recommends exercising just before lunch. This specific timing is beneficial because it improves the way the body processes the subsequent meal.
6.  **Beginner vs. Advanced Ratios:** The Beginner level utilizes 20 seconds of exercise followed by 40 seconds of rest. In contrast, the Advanced level increases the intensity and reduces recovery time with 40 seconds of exercise and only 20 seconds of rest.
7.  **Simple Sweat Modification:** If a person finds certain exercises too difficult or has an injury, they can substitute movements or repeat a single exercise they enjoy. The "Simple Sweat" version involves choosing just one of the five exercises and repeating it for all five prescribed intervals.
8.  **Mountain Climber Alignment:** The body should be perfectly aligned in a traditional push-up position with the head, shoulders, and bottom forming a straight line. The pelvis should be tucked under to ensure the arch in the lower back disappears.
9.  **Push-up Form and Safety:** Elbows should be kept tucked in during the movement rather than flaring out to the sides. Flaring the elbows is discouraged because it places excessive strain on the shoulders.
10. **Sumo Squat Mechanics:** Feet should be placed slightly wider than hip-width apart and turned out slightly. During the descent, the weight should be concentrated in the heels while the chest remains up and out.

## Part 4: Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **HIIT** | High Intensity Interval Training; a workout method consisting of short periods of intense exercise alternated with recovery periods. |
| **Insulin** | A hormone in the body; HIIT improves sensitivity to this hormone, lowering the risk of type 2 diabetes. |
| **Mitochondria** | The microscopic "power-packs" within cells that generate the energy necessary for the body to function. |
| **Mountain Climbers** | A fast-paced exercise performed from a push-up position, involving alternating knees toward the chest while maintaining a straight body line. |
| **Power 5** | A specific five-minute workout routine consisting of Jogging on the Spot, Jumping Jacks, Mountain Climbers, Push-ups, and Sumo Squats. |
| **Simple Sweat** | A modified version of the Power 5 workout where a participant repeats a single preferred exercise for all intervals. |
| **Sumo Squats** | A lower-body exercise performed with a wide stance and feet turned out, mimicking the sitting motion to target the bottom and thighs. |
| **TOFI** | An acronym for "Thin on the Outside, Fat on the Inside," describing individuals with low visible fat but high internal visceral fat. |
| **Visceral Fat** | Invisible fat that stores around internal organs; it is linked to strokes, heart attacks, and diabetes. |
