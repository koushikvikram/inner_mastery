# Epictetus Operating Model — Daniel Priestley, *Becoming a Key Person of Influence*

> Source frame: most professionals are living out a **20-year decision a teenager made** — "professional baggage" from an Industrial Age that no longer exists. The way out is to move from the **outer circle** (80% of people fighting for 10% of the wealth) to the **inner circle** of the **Key Person of Influence (KPI)**. The decisive split is **Functional vs. Vital**: Functional people execute process and fear holidays (because the process continues without them — they’re obsolete); **Vital** people own the *result*, and their absence makes the industry worry. Stop being a **Swagman** (jack-of-all-trades wandering between farms); **stake a Micro-Niche claim**. Don’t mistake the **microphone for the singer** — *a bad singer with a microphone is a louder bad singer*. Run the strict five-step **KPI Method** in order: **Pitch → Publish → Product → Profile → Partnership**. Stop "working hard" — that’s a factory legacy; play a **Big Game** (Rules / Players / Prize / Way to win). Crystallize the **Mountain of Value** you’re already standing on (escape "Glass House Mountains" syndrome where every peak looks equal because you’re on yours).

## Layer 1 — Practice (What must be done?)

### What we’re trying to achieve
- Move from the **outer circle** (functional, replaceable) to the **inner circle** (vital, irreplaceable, in-demand).
- Crystallize your **Mountain of Value** into IP that works while you sleep.
- Claim a defensible **Micro-Niche** — sniper rifle, not shotgun.
- Run the **KPI Method** in order — never skip steps.
- Replace **hard work** with playing a **Big Game**.

### Non-negotiable behaviors
- **Audit your professional baggage** — discard 20-year-old teenager decisions.
- **Choose Vital over Functional** in every role decision.
- **Pick one Micro-Niche** and turn down general work that pulls you off it.
- **Run KPI in sequence**: Pitch → Publish → Product → Profile → Partnership.
- **Frame work as a Big Game** with rules, players, prize, win/lose.

### Routines / checklists

- **Baggage audit (quarterly)**
  - List the major career decisions still shaping your life.
  - Tag each: *teenager-era / 5-yr-old / current.*
  - Discard or rewrite anything that no longer fits the digital, intangible economy.

- **Vital vs. Functional check (monthly)**
  - For each part of your role:
    - **Functional sign**: focus on process, busy-ness, anxiety about being replaced, holidays feel threatening.
    - **Vital sign**: focus on *result*, "why" / "what if" thinking, holidays come back richer.
  - Rebalance toward Vital; outsource or automate Functional surface area.

- **The "Holiday Test" (annually)**
  - Honestly answer: *if I disappeared for a month, would my industry worry I might not come back, or quietly find a cheaper replacement?*
  - The answer drives the next quarter’s focus.

- **Micro-Niche staking (one-time, then defended)**
  - Define: **For [specific audience], I solve [specific problem] using [specific method].**
  - Examples (echoing the source): *Facebook ads for hair salons; body transformations for busy city executives; testimonial videos for estate agents.*
  - Test: a stranger should be able to repeat your niche in one sentence.
  - Refuse general work that breaks the niche, even when it pays.

- **KPI 5-step ladder (the order is the protocol)**
  - **1) Pitch**
    - 60-sec social pitch + 5–10 min presentation pitch.
    - Define your **Big Game**: rules, players, prize, way to win/lose.
    - Test on real audiences; iterate based on emotional response, not content density.
  - **2) Publish**
    - One signature long-form asset (book, white paper, signature talk).
    - Steady stream of articles or essays — *"Almost having a book" means zero books on Amazon.*
    - Authority comes from the artifact, not the intention.
  - **3) Product**
    - Convert your IP into an ecosystem: digital product / productized service / implementation engagement.
    - Each product should serve a different stage of the customer journey.
  - **4) Profile**
    - Build a Google-able presence: website, podcast appearances, owned channels.
    - Periodic press / traditional media appearances.
    - Audit: what comes up when someone searches your name?
  - **5) Partnership**
    - Now — only now — pursue joint ventures, affiliate plays, channel partners.
    - Win/win spirit; selective, not promiscuous.
  - **Order discipline**: Partnership without Profile is a beggar; Profile without Publish is hollow; Publish without Pitch is shouting into the void.

- **Microphone-vs-Singer check (before any platform investment)**
  - Before buying ads, growth tools, social media courses — ask:
    - Is the **song** ready? (Pitch + Publish complete?)
    - If not, the microphone amplifies a bad singer. Postpone the spend; finish the song first.

- **Mountain of Value extraction (quarterly, 60 min)**
  - List everything you’ve done that produced unusual results.
  - Distill into 3–5 reusable principles or frameworks (your IP).
  - This is the raw material of Publish + Product steps.

- **Big Game design (per major project / year)**
  - **Rules**: structure, timeframe, constraints.
  - **Players**: who is on the team? Who else needs to play?
  - **Prize**: what makes it emotionally exciting (recognition, impact, wealth)?
  - **Way to win / lose**: clear goalposts that produce energy.
  - If you can’t describe the game — you’re grinding, not playing.

### What good execution looks like
- A stranger can describe your micro-niche in one sentence after 30 seconds with you.
- The KPI ladder is climbed in order; you can name your current rung honestly.
- Your name produces a real footprint when searched (book / talk / articles / press).
- A holiday makes your industry worry, not relax.
- You can describe today’s work as a Big Game with all 4 elements present.
- Your Mountain of Value has been crystallized into reusable IP.

## Layer 2 — Principles (Why does it work?)

- **Yesterday’s decisions are today’s liabilities** in a world that no longer rewards them.
- **Functional = replaceable**: process is exactly what software, AI, and offshore vendors specialize in.
- **Vital = irreplaceable**: creativity, judgment, vision, and the unique result you produce.
- **Narrowing produces notoriety**: "Key Person of *all sorts of things*" doesn’t exist.
- **Order matters in influence-building**: each KPI step compounds the next; skipping is expensive.
- **Microphones don’t fix songs**: distribution is downstream of value.
- **Hard work is a factory legacy**: vitality and Big-Game framing produce 10x outcomes from less effort.
- **You’re standing on a Mountain of Value already**: the work isn’t to climb a new mountain, it’s to crystallize the one you’re on.

### Decision rules (doctrine)
- If your current path was chosen by a younger version of you, audit it now.
- If you fear holidays, you’re Functional — restructure toward Vital.
- If your niche can’t fit in one sentence, narrow until it does.
- If you’re investing in distribution before authority, you’re amplifying a bad singer.
- If you can’t name the rules, players, prize, and win/lose — it’s not a Big Game, it’s a grind.
- If a generalist opportunity tugs at you, ask: *does this make me a Swagman again?*

## Layer 3 — Meta (How do we evaluate and improve?)

### Metrics (leading, behavior-tied)
- **Vital-time %**: hours spent on result-focused work vs. process-focused work.
- **KPI ladder rung** (current step + visible deliverables).
- **Niche-clarity test**: % of new contacts who repeat your niche correctly after one minute.
- **Google-ability score**: top 5 search results when someone searches your name (annual).
- **Big Game count / year**: explicit games designed and played.
- **Microphone-spend ratio**: how much spent on amplification vs. how much built in song (Pitch + Publish).
- **Holiday Test annual rating** (1–5: would the industry worry?).

### Review loop
- **Weekly**: one Vital-vs-Functional sweep; one rung-progress action.
- **Quarterly**: Mountain of Value extraction; Niche audit; Baggage audit.
- **Annually**: Holiday Test; redesign the Big Game.

### Core rule guardrail
- If you’re skipping the order of the KPI Method — chasing Profile before Publishing, or building Partnerships before having a Product — the sequence collapses and the work feels like noise. Restart at the highest unfinished rung. The order *is* the protocol; it isn’t decorative.
